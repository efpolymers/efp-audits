Section 01 — Executive Summary

Department Overview

Scope, current state, and headline findings

The CBDO Office manages strategic growth across 4 legacy domestic verticals (Seed, B2B/Company, NGO/CSR, Government) and 1 New Business vertical (Cocopeat & Japan Exports). The team is highly specialized, driving sophisticated institutional sales cycles.

However, the audit reveals a critical operational vulnerability: the complete absence of a central CRM. Active deal pipelines and client communication histories are siloed within the personal WhatsApp accounts of individual executives. The CBDO has no live visibility into sales performance, relying instead on manual 1:1 meetings and retrospective monthly reports to gauge pipeline health. Implementing a robust CRM (e.g., Zoho/HubSpot) is the urgent priority to prevent lead leakage and unlock scale.

Section 02 — Risk & Control Findings

Findings Register

Ranked by risk rating. Each finding is cross-referenced to its recommendation in Section 04.
IDFindingRiskEvidence / ImpactLinked Rec.
F-01 Absence of Central CRM. Lead status and ownership are invisible at the executive level. Critical Requires manual meetings to determine account owners; risks massive lead leakage if personnel transition. R-01
F-02 Retrospective Monthly Reporting. Pipeline reporting is only conducted retrospectively once a month. Medium Prevents timely CBDO intervention on stalled deals; obscures live revenue forecasting. R-02
F-03 Siloed WhatsApp Communications. Customer interactions live exclusively inside employees' personal WhatsApp accounts. Medium No central capture of client history or frequency of touchpoints. R-01
F-04 Process Disparities. Cocopeat uses a basic tracker, while the 4 legacy domestic verticals have no formal logging. Low Inconsistent tracking discipline across the department creates friction during cross-vertical reporting. R-03
The CBDO Office’s findings follow a clear structural chain: the absence of a central CRM (F-01, Critical) is the root cause of both siloed WhatsApp communication (F-03) and retrospective reporting (F-02). Fixing F-01 inherently addresses F-03 — they share recommendation R-01. The Low-rated process disparity (F-04) is deliberately placed in Phase 1 as a zero-cost training exercise: enforcing the Cocopeat tracker across all verticals builds the data-entry muscle memory required before the CRM rollout in Phase 2.
