Section 02

Existing Workflows

Workflow 1: Pipeline Status Review

Real-time visibility into active pipelines is limited. Account ownership, deal stages, and interaction histories are not aggregated. The CBDO must conduct manual one-on-one meetings with each vertical lead to understand deal status.

→

Lead checks personal spreadsheet

Workflow 2: Monthly Performance Reporting

Performance is tracked via retrospective monthly status reports. Verticals categorize leads by sales stage and type to present to the CBDO. This is backward-looking — preventing timely intervention on stalled deals.

Vertical leads compile monthly stats
→
Retrospective (last month's data)
→
CBDO reviews performance
→
Stalled deals only discovered after 30+ days

Workflow 3: Lead Sourcing Strategy (Per Vertical)

The CBDO has intentionally rejected a unified lead generation process. Each vertical operates a distinct, tailored sourcing workflow. While this accommodates different target demographics, it means no cross-vertical visibility.

Seed: Personal networks + ChatGPT research
NGO: LinkedIn feed scrolling (9,500 connections)
B2B/RCM: WhatsApp communities + field training
Govt: Government trial databases + field ops
Cocopeat: Google/LinkedIn Ads + cold email

Each vertical's sourcing method is independent. No shared lead database or cross-vertical insights exist.

Lead status and ownership are completely invisible at the executive level, requiring manual meetings to determine account owners or deal stages. This is the single largest operational risk across the organization.

Section 04

Information Gaps & Cross-Department Leaks

⚠

CBDO ↔ All BD Verticals (Pipeline Ownership)

The CBDO cannot determine who owns which lead or what stage a deal is at without scheduling a meeting. This means pipeline conflicts (two reps contacting the same company) can occur undetected.

⚠

CBDO ↔ Logistics (Export Friction)

The CBDO assesses minimal cross-department friction for domestic operations, but the Cocopeat export vertical faces a complex dependency chain (Accounts → Logistics → Documentation → R&D). This disparity is not visible at the CBDO level.

⚠

CBDO ↔ COO (Seed Vertical Dual Reporting)

The Seed vertical has dual reporting lines to both the COO and CBDO, creating task prioritization and alignment risks. Neither executive has full visibility into which directives the Seed BD Manager is executing.

⚠

Across All Verticals (Customer Data Fragmentation)

Customer data lives in 5+ separate spreadsheets, personal WhatsApp threads, and email inboxes. No unified customer master exists. Accounts, Logistics, and Sales each maintain separate records.

Approach: Implement Zoho CRM across all 5 BD verticals and MENA. Phase 1: Consolidated lead database + deal stage tracking. Phase 2: Custom templates per vertical. Zoho CRM aligns with the existing Zoho ecosystem (Books, Inventory, Expense) — no new vendor needed.

Solution for: Retrospective Monthly Reporting

Approach: CRM dashboards provide real-time pipeline views automatically. No separate reporting action needed — the CBDO opens the dashboard and sees live deal stages, stalled deals, and conversion rates per vertical.

PriorityEffortApproachExpected Savings
P1Included in CRMBuilt-in Zoho CRM dashboardsStalled deals caught in days not months; 3–5 hrs/week of review time optimized

Section 07

Additional Optimization Suggestions

💡 Cross-Vertical Lead Sharing Mechanism

While each vertical has distinct sourcing, a company that doesn't fit one vertical might be a perfect lead for another. A simple CRM tag-and-transfer workflow would enable cross-vertical lead sharing without merging sourcing strategies.

Priority: P2Effort: Minimal (CRM feature)Impact: Prevents lead waste

💡 Unified Customer Master Database

Accounts, BD, and Logistics each maintain separate customer records. A shared Customer Master in Zoho CRM (synced with Zoho Books) would eliminate repeated data requests and reduce invoicing errors.

Priority: P1 (part of CRM)Effort: Included in CRMImpact: Eliminates data duplication

💡 Weekly Pipeline Pulse (Automated)

Instead of monthly retrospective reviews, configure CRM to send a weekly automated "Pipeline Pulse" email to the CBDO — showing new leads, stalled deals, deals closing this week, and conversion trends. Zero manual effort required.

Priority: P2Effort: Minimal (CRM config)Impact: Proactive deal management
Section 08

Phased Roadmap

Phase 1 — Quick Wins (Highest ROI)

Zoho CRM Phase 1: Lead Database + Deal Stages

Migrate all BD verticals to Zoho CRM. Import existing spreadsheet data. Configure deal stages per vertical. 100% pipeline visibility from day one.

Customer Master Database Setup

Unified customer records shared between BD, Accounts, and Logistics. Eliminates repeated data requests.

Device/SIM Ownership Policy

All customer-facing roles transition to company-owned SIMs. Protects customer relationships.

Where this places us: For the first time, the CBDO has real-time visibility into every deal across every vertical. Customer data has a single source of truth. Contact continuity is protected.
Phase 2 — Operational Foundation

CRM Phase 2: Custom Templates + Automation

Build vertical-specific CRM templates, automated follow-up reminders, and weekly Pipeline Pulse reports. Eliminates memory-based follow-ups.

Resolve Dual Reporting (Seed Vertical)

Define RACI matrix. Primary = CBDO for commercial, COO for operational. Ends prioritization conflicts.

Cross-Vertical Lead Transfer Workflow

Enable tag-and-transfer in CRM so misfit leads flow to the right vertical. Prevents lead waste.

Where this places us: BD operations are systematized. Follow-ups are automated. Deals don't fall through cracks. Cross-vertical intelligence begins flowing. Maturity jumps from Level 1 to Level 2.5+.
Phase 3 — Advanced Optimization

CRM Analytics + Predictive Insights

Zoho CRM analytics for conversion rate benchmarking, revenue forecasting, and vertical performance comparison. Data-driven sales strategy.

CRM ↔ Zoho Books Integration

Auto-generate invoices from CRM deal closures. Seamless quote-to-cash workflow. Eliminates manual invoicing handoffs.

Where this places us: The Sales & BD function operates at Level 3+ maturity. Pipeline-to-cash is automated. The CBDO operates on data, not verbal updates. Sales strategy is evidence-driven.
