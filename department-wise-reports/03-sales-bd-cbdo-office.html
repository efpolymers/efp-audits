Section 01 — Executive Summary

Department Overview

Scope, current state, and headline findings

The CBDO Office manages strategic growth across 4 legacy domestic verticals (Seed, B2B/Company, NGO/CSR, Government) and 1 New Business vertical (Cocopeat & Japan Exports). The team is highly specialized, driving sophisticated institutional sales cycles.

However, the audit reveals a critical operational vulnerability: the complete absence of a central CRM. Active deal pipelines and client communication histories are siloed within the personal WhatsApp accounts of individual executives. The CBDO has no live visibility into sales performance, relying instead on manual 1:1 meetings and retrospective monthly reports to gauge pipeline health. Implementing a robust CRM (e.g., Zoho/HubSpot) is the urgent priority to prevent lead leakage and unlock scale.

Section 06 — Corrective Action Plan

Recommended Solution Segment

This section outlines the integrated technology solution proposed to resolve operational bottlenecks, replacing the legacy recommendations register.

Implementation Required

Solution Modules to be Built:

1
Central CRM & Lead Ownership
  • CRM Phase 1 Deployment: Deploy a central CRM (e.g., Zoho/HubSpot) requiring all executives to log deal stages, ensuring 100% lead ownership visibility at the executive level.
  • WhatsApp Integration: Migrate all client interactions from personal WhatsApp accounts into the corporate CRM database with automated activity logging.
  • Vertical-Specific Templates: Configure CRM templates for Seed, B2B, NGO, Government, and Cocopeat verticals with standardized pipeline stages.
2
Live Executive Dashboarding
  • Real-Time Pipeline View: Shift from manual monthly retrospective reviews to live CRM dashboards monitoring pipeline health daily.
  • Stalled Deal Alerts: Automated alerts flag any deal with no activity for 7+ days directly to the CBDO.
  • Conversion Analytics: Track deal velocity, conversion ratios, and cross-vertical benchmarking in real time.
3
Cross-Vertical Process Standardization
  • Unified Tracker Framework: Deploy the Cocopeat vertical's Excel tracking framework across all 4 legacy domestic verticals with unified column definitions as a stop-gap before CRM launch.
  • Standardized Pipeline Stages: Define uniform stage labels (Prospect, Qualified, Proposal, Negotiation, Closed) to align cross-vertical reporting and build data-entry discipline.

What Remains the Same

  • Sales Methodology: The BD team's relationship-driven approach remains unchanged; the CRM augments rather than replaces it.
  • Vertical Autonomy: Each vertical retains its operational independence within the unified CRM framework.

Tech Stack Utilized

  • CRM: Zoho CRM / HubSpot
  • Dashboard: ReactJS + TypeScript
  • Integration: WhatsApp Business API, Gmail
  • Host: Google Cloud Platform

Key Operational Problems Solved

  • Eliminates lead leakage risk during employee turnover by securing corporate ownership of all contact data.
  • Replaces monthly retrospective reviews with live pipeline visibility, enabling timely CBDO intervention on stalled deals.
  • Centralizes all client interactions from personal WhatsApp accounts into a single auditable CRM database.
  • Standardizes tracking discipline across all 5 BD verticals, eliminating cross-vertical reporting friction.

Future Scope of Improvement / Enhancement

  • AI Deal Scoring: In the future, an AI model could analyze CRM data to score deal health, predict win probability, and recommend next-best-actions for each BD executive. This is not needed right now but is a planned future enhancement.

Next Steps for Team Adoption

  • Mandatory CRM Training: All BD executives must undergo compulsory CRM data entry training with vertical-specific templates before the system goes live.
  • Feedback Loop: Establish weekly 15-minute pipeline stand-ups to instill data-entry discipline and iterate on the CRM workflow design based on ground feedback.
