Section 03 — Process Detail

Current Workflows

Analysis of current operational sequences

Sales & Deployment Lifecycle

What is good: The Monday-prioritized cold email cadence shows process discipline. The 4-tab Google Sheet architecture provides a basic but functional funnel view from sourced leads through to active meetings.

What needs to be fixed: Follow-ups every 4–5 days are tracked by memory from a spreadsheet — zero automation. A CRM with automatic cadence triggers is urgently needed given 40–50 active leads.

Bottlenecks: The collections step is the most severe bottleneck — the 60–70 day NGO payment cycle forces AM-BD Lead Vikash Mehta to dedicate 15–20% of his time to manual payment chasing, directly competing with pipeline growth activities.

Dependencies: Finance/Accounts must trigger payment reminders in sync with BD. Field team logistics for pilot dispatches and travel depend on Logistics department. Both are entirely manual today.

Appendix — Cross-Department Dependencies

Cross-Department Dependencies

Findings requiring action from other departments to resolve.
Finance & Accounts
Critical Gating — Collections
Operations Dependency
The 60–70 day NGO payment cycle is absorbed manually by BD. Vikash Mehta spends 15–20% of his time chasing overdue invoices instead of managing pipeline. No automated dunning or payment reminder sequence exists.
👤 Key Person: Shobha Shekhawat (Finance)
Critical Bottleneck
This is the single biggest drain on BD leadership capacity in this vertical. Manual chasing is reactive and inconsistent — with no systematic escalation path if a client ignores reminders.
Action Needed
Finance must own the dunning process: automated 30/45/60-day payment reminders triggered from invoice date. BD should only be looped in at the 60+ day escalation point, not from day one.
Logistics
Pilot Dispatch Support
Operations Dependency
Bulk trial materials must be dispatched and tracked in coordination with the Field Execution Team's travel schedule. Delays in dispatch delay trial setup, directly costing deal velocity.
👤 Key Person: Nihal Paliwal (Logistics)
Critical Bottleneck
Trial dispatch is treated as a transactional ad-hoc request with no dedicated SLA. If field team members travel to site without materials arriving on time, the demo cannot proceed and must be rescheduled.
Action Needed
Logistics must commit to a maximum dispatch lead time for NGO trial materials. BD should be able to track shipment status in real time, not rely on phone calls to Nihal for status updates.
