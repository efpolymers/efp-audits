Section 01 — Executive Summary

Report Overview — NGO/CSR

Scope, current state, and headline findings

The NGO/CSR vertical secures high-volume bulk contracts with non-governmental organizations and corporate CSR departments managing agricultural and watershed projects. The vertical carries a 100 MT sales target with a 4-person active team supported by a vacant executive position.

The audit identified three compounding failure points: a memory-based follow-up cadence (every 4–5 days, tracked manually from a 4-tab Google Sheet), LinkedIn feed-only sourcing with no structured lead capture, and a 60–70 day receivables cycle that pulls the AM-BD Lead into manual payment chasing. Together these constraints cap the vertical at 1.4 maturity against a target of 3.0.

Section 03 — Process Detail

Current Workflows

Where each finding sits inside the operational process

Lead Sourcing & Enrichment Lifecycle — P-03

What is good: Relevancy filter (agri / water only) is well-defined, and Contactout + Apollo AI extensions accelerate contact discovery once a POC is identified.

What needs to be fixed: Sourcing is restricted to organic feed scrolling — no keyword search, no Sales Navigator, no conference lead capture pipeline. Volume is capped by Gaurav's reading time.

Bottlenecks: No structured intent-search; no capture of leads from conferences, online trainings, or referrals into a single intake.

Dependencies: Single-person dependency on Gaurav's LinkedIn network and his personal feed curation.

Outreach & Memory-Based Follow-Up Cadence — P-02 P-04

What is good: Monday-prioritized cold email cadence and Saturday avoidance show process discipline. 4-tab Sheet architecture provides a basic funnel view.

What needs to be fixed: Follow-ups are triggered by memory every 4–5 days. With 40–50 active leads, slippage is inevitable. No automated reminder exists.

Bottlenecks: Manual Sheet check before every follow-up mail. Each lead's status is reconstructed by reading the Sheet, not surfaced proactively.

Dependencies: Single-person execution — Gaurav owns sourcing, emailing, follow-ups, and meeting scheduling.

Virtual Meeting → Pilot → Conversion — P-05 P-07

What is good: The meeting → pilot → training → conversion sequence is well-defined and consistently executed.

What needs to be fixed: Each custom deck is built fresh in Canva/PPT — no template library, no reusable partnership framework. Pilot dispatch is treated as an ad-hoc Logistics request with no SLA.

Bottlenecks: Deck creation consumes ~20% of BD time. Logistics dispatch delays directly cost deal velocity if field team arrives without materials.

Dependencies: Field Team (2 personnel) executes the demo & training; Logistics (Nihal Paliwal) handles dispatch. Both coordinations are manual today.

4-Sheet Pipeline Data Flow & Aggregation — P-04 P-08

What is good: Sequential 4-tab architecture provides a logical funnel from sourced leads to active meetings.

What needs to be fixed: Tabs are not cross-referenced. There is no live view of where any lead sits in the funnel — Vikash must manually open and scan each sheet.

Bottlenecks: Monthly metric reporting to Jatin is assembled by hand from these sheets plus a separate past-year purchase tracker and a dispatch tracker. No automated aggregation.

Dependencies: Reporting depends on Gaurav and Field Team updating sheets promptly; delays cascade into Vikash's monthly submission.

Order Fulfilment & Collections Cycle — P-01

What is good: Dispatch confirmation flows via WhatsApp and mail — both channels are already in daily use by the client.

What needs to be fixed: No automated payment reminder sequence. The 60–70 day NGO cycle is absorbed by Vikash manually, with no escalation path.

Bottlenecks: Vikash spends 15–20% of his time chasing overdue invoices. This directly competes with strategic account management and pipeline leadership.

Dependencies: Finance/Accounts (Shobha Shekhawat) owns invoice data but does not trigger reminders. This is a prime cross-departmental integration opportunity.

Agronomy Newsletter & Update Broadcast — P-06

What is good: Vikash actively maintains relationship touchpoints through direct WhatsApp contact with key accounts.

What needs to be fixed: No broadcast tool — each forward is manual. Cadence is irregular ("when something arrives"). No tracking of opens or engagement.

Bottlenecks: 20–30 manual forwards per update. Skipped when Vikash is busy with collections chasing, causing passive reach decay.

Dependencies: Sole ownership by Vikash. WhatsApp Business API broadcast would resolve this without changing the recipient experience.

Section 06 — Corrective Action Plan

Recommended Solution Segment

This section outlines the integrated technology solution proposed to resolve operational bottlenecks, replacing the legacy recommendations register. Built on the Solution Design Framework — extend existing tools, not replace them.

Implementation Required

Solution Modules to be Built:

1
BD Pipeline Dashboard (Extend)
  • Bi-Directional Sheet Sync: Connects directly to the existing 4 Google Sheets. The team keeps using Sheets exactly as today — but the dashboard provides Vikash a live consolidated funnel view (Generated → Emailed → Responded → Meetings) without manual aggregation.
  • Automated Follow-up Cadence: Replaces memory-based 4–5 day checks. Each lead row carries a "last contacted" timestamp; the system triggers WhatsApp reminders to Gaurav when silence exceeds the threshold. Zero new tool to adopt.
  • Leadership Visibility: Jatin gets a real-time pipeline view — no more waiting for Vikash's monthly manual aggregation. Conversion rates, aging leads, and dispatch status all surfaced live.
2
AI Lead Discovery & Proposal Assistant (Custom Build)
  • LinkedIn Feed Scanner: An AI layer that monitors Gaurav's LinkedIn feed and auto-flags posts matching agri/water CSR keywords. Eliminates manual scrolling; surfaces 3× more relevant leads without changing the sourcing channel.
  • Proposal Drafting Engine: Custom AI assistant built on Gemini that reads existing past proposals stored on Drive and auto-generates a customized first draft per NGO program alignment. Lives inside the dashboard — no app-jumping.
  • Multi-Source Capture: Structured intake for conference leads, online training referrals, and incoming references — all routed into Sheet 1 automatically.
3
Cross-Departmental Automated Communications (Integration)
  • Finance-Triggered Dunning: Cross-departmental integration with Accounts. Automated 30/45/60-day payment reminders triggered from invoice date via WhatsApp (client) and Gmail (formal). Vikash is looped in only at the 60+ day escalation point — not from day one.
  • WhatsApp Broadcast API: Replaces 1-by-1 manual forwards. Agronomy newsletters and updates sent to the full 20–30 contact list simultaneously, with open/read tracking.
  • Logistics Dispatch Sync: Pilot dispatch status pushed via WhatsApp to BD when materials ship, with ETA. Field team travel synced to dispatch confirmation — no more phone calls to Nihal.

What Remains the Same

  • No CRM Migration: The 4 Google Sheets remain the system of record. Team continues using Sheets exactly as today.
  • Familiar Channels: WhatsApp, Gmail, and Google Meet remain the primary communication tools. No new app for the client to learn.

Tech Stack Utilized

  • Frontend: ReactJS + TypeScript
  • APIs: Google Sheets, WhatsApp Business, Gmail
  • AI: Gemini LLM (feed scanning + proposal drafting)
  • Host: Google Cloud Platform

Key Operational Problems Solved

  • Eliminates 15–20% of Vikash's time on manual payment chasing via Finance-triggered dunning sequence.
  • Removes memory-based follow-up cadence — automated WhatsApp reminders replace manual Sheet scanning every 4–5 days.
  • Scales sourcing beyond one person's feed-reading capacity via AI lead discovery layer.
  • Provides Jatin and CXOs instant pipeline visibility without waiting for monthly manual aggregation.
  • Resolves manual WhatsApp broadcast bottleneck with bulk API + open tracking.

Future Scope of Improvement / Enhancement

  • AI Pipeline Health Agent: In the future, an AI agent could actively monitor pipeline velocity, flag stale leads, suggest next-best-actions, and auto-draft follow-up emails based on context. Not needed in Phase 1 but planned as a future enhancement once the dashboard is adopted.
  • CRM Graduation: Once the team is comfortable with the dashboard layer, a phased migration to a full CRM (e.g., HubSpot) can be evaluated — but only after adoption is proven.

Next Steps for Team Adoption

  • Mandatory Training: Gaurav and Vikash must complete walkthrough sessions on the dashboard and AI proposal assistant. Adoption training is compulsory before Phase 2 rollout.
  • Feedback Loop: Establish a continuous feedback loop to iterate on the solution on the ground, ensuring it genuinely adapts to the BD team's operational reality.
  • Fill Vacant BD Executive: The planned hiring should proceed in parallel — the dashboard is built to absorb additional sourcing capacity seamlessly.
