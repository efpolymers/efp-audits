Section 01 — Executive Summary

Department Overview

Scope, current state, and headline findings

The Finance & Accounts team manages corporate bookkeeping, regulatory tax filing, cost accounting, and employee expense auditing. The core 3-person team relies heavily on the Zoho Suite, but encounters massive friction in cross-system data transfer.

The audit revealed a severe structural issue in cost accounting: COGS margins fluctuate wildly between 50% and 77% month-to-month due to the absence of grade-based Work-in-Progress (WIP) valuation in Zoho Inventory. Furthermore, Accounts lacks real-time stock visibility for invoicing, requiring manual phone calls to Production for every dispatch, and must spend 1–2 weeks manually reconciling GST ledgers.

Section 02 — Risk & Control Findings

Findings Register

Ranked by risk rating. Each finding is cross-referenced to its recommendation in Section 04.
IDFindingRiskEvidence / ImpactLinked Rec.
F-01 Untraceable COGS Margin Volatility. COGS margins swing 50%–77% monthly because Zoho Inventory cannot value Work-in-Progress (WIP) or grade-based costs. Critical Executive leadership cannot rely on the P&L margin logic; distorts profitability. R-01
F-02 Manual Tax Compliance Load. Reconciling government GST portals against Zoho Books takes 1–2 weeks of manual labor every month. Critical Burns 35% of the team's monthly capacity on non-strategic data entry. R-02
F-03 Lack of Real-time Stock Visibility. Accounts cannot check stock levels before invoicing, requiring manual phone calls to Production. Medium Creates an invoicing bottleneck; prevents seamless order dispatch. R-03
F-04 Manual Sales Quantity Compilation. Zoho Books reports sales by value, not by tonnage/SKUs. Medium Accounts must download and manually compile volume metrics in Excel. R-04
Two findings are rated Critical and both severely impact corporate decision-making and compliance. F-01 (COGS Volatility) distorts the fundamental profitability metrics used by executive leadership, as swings of 50–77% make margin analysis impossible. F-02 (Tax Compliance) is a massive resource drain, consuming 35% of the team's capacity on tasks that modern ERPs should automate. Phase 1 targets the immediate manual bottlenecks (tax reconciliation and stock checking) before addressing the more complex WIP costing architecture in Phase 2.

Section 04 — Corrective Action Plan

Recommendations Register

Ordered by priority. Effort and impact are directional estimates for planning purposes.
IDRecommendationFindingPriorityEffortExpected Impact
R-01 Zoho Inventory WIP Costing — Implement grade-based costing and WIP tracking layers within Zoho Inventory to capture exact COGS per production batch. F-01 P1 High Eliminates 50-77% margin variance; allows leadership to trust the P&L.
R-02 Automated Bank & GST Reconciliation Rules — Configure Zoho Books custom rules and bank feeds to auto-match invoices against incoming bank settlements and GST portals. F-02 P1 Medium Saves 1–2 weeks of manual labor; reduces tax audit risk.
R-03 Real-time Stock Visibility — Enable Accounts to view live stock availability natively within Zoho before drafting invoices. F-03 P2 Low Removes phone-call bottleneck, accelerating order-to-invoice time.
R-04 Zoho Books Tonnage Reporting — Develop custom views in Zoho Analytics/Books to export sales volumes by metric tons (MT), bypassing value-only limits. F-04 P2 Medium Eliminates manual excel data compilation for operational reporting.
R-05 Shared Master Database — Establish a central Customer/Vendor MDM ensuring Logistics, Sales, and Accounts share synchronized GST and address data. Data Silo P3 High Prevents repetitive master data checking when raising invoices.
