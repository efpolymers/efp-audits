Section 01 — Executive Summary

Department Overview

Scope, current state, and headline findings

The Logistics department, managed by Nihal Paliwal, is a lean, two-person team handling the entirety of EF Polymers' domestic dispatch and complex international export schedules.

The core audit finding is severe upstream order friction. Sales representatives trigger orders via unstructured WhatsApp text messages that frequently lack mandatory fields (e.g., pin codes, SKU weights). This forces the Logistics Manager into an administrative chase, extending standard call durations to 20–25 minutes per order. Furthermore, tracking spreadsheets remain entirely disconnected from the Zoho Books billing engine, necessitating manual double-entry.

Section 02 — Risk & Control Findings

Findings Register

Ranked by risk rating. Each finding is cross-referenced to its recommendation in Section 04.
IDFindingRiskEvidence / ImpactLinked Rec.
F-01 Incomplete WhatsApp Order Intake. Orders are entered via WhatsApp text with missing fields (pin codes, SKU weight). Critical Forces Logistics to spend 10-25 mins manually chasing BD for missing compliance data. R-01
F-02 Disconnected Invoicing & Shipping Sheets. Zoho Books and the logistics tracking sheet operate as separate silos. Critical Requires double data entry for every shipment; risks typing errors in billing vs tracking. R-02
F-03 Ocean Freight Schedule Shifts. Vessel schedule shifts and cutoff delays require manual tracking. Medium Introduces export delays if notifications are missed during high-volume periods. R-03
F-04 Factory Single-Line Capacity. The Coimbatore plant runs a single production line. Low Peak export schedules directly delay concurrent domestic fulfillment. R-04
The two Critical findings pinpoint massive administrative waste caused by poor data intake and disconnected systems. F-01 (WhatsApp Orders) forces the Logistics Manager to spend up to 30% of their time chasing basic data, artificially slowing standard dispatch turnaround. F-02 (Disconnected Sheets) creates severe double-entry risks across financial and shipping records. Phase 1 targets the root cause by entirely eliminating unstructured WhatsApp orders in favor of rigid intake forms.

Section 04 — Corrective Action Plan

Recommendations Register

Ordered by priority. Effort and impact are directional estimates for planning purposes.
IDRecommendationFindingPriorityEffortExpected Impact
R-01 Mandatory Order Intake Forms — Force Sales to use a Zoho or Google Form that physically rejects submissions if pin codes, tax IDs, and exact SKUs are missing. F-01 P1 Low Reclaims up to 15% of the Manager's time wasted on chasing basic compliance data.
R-02 Zoho Books - Logistics API Sync — Configure a direct export from Zoho Books into the Logistics Google Sheet to auto-populate shipping matrices. F-02 P1 Medium Ends double-entry between the Accounts ERP and dispatch scheduling.
R-03 Automated Carrier API Alerts — Integrate carrier vessel tracking APIs to ping the Logistics channel when cutoff times shift dynamically. F-03 P2 Medium Prevents export container misses; auto-notifies international clients.
R-04 Multi-Line Factory Capacity Expansion — Evaluate Capex for parallel processing lines to isolate export batching from domestic continuous flow. F-04 P3 High Strategic capacity scale-up removes internal production bottlenecks during peak ocean freight windows.
