Section 01 — Executive Summary

Department Overview

Scope, current state, and headline findings

The Logistics department, led by Nihal M Paliwal, manages the full dispatch lifecycle for EF Polymer Private Limited — covering domestic B2B and RCM orders daily, international export shipments of 10–15 orders per month, and the custom packaging pipeline for white-label B2B clients.

The audit identified three interconnected friction points pulling down operational efficiency. First, 70–80% of domestic sales orders arrive via unstructured WhatsApp messages with missing mandatory fields, forcing the Logistics Manager into manual back-and-forth clarification before billing can begin. Second, Zoho Books (invoicing) and the Google Sheets tracking system are entirely siloed — every shipment requires double data entry across both systems. Third, the custom packaging lead time of 15–20 days with no upstream sales forecast creates recurring risk of missing export vessel windows.

Section 03 — Process Detail

Current Workflows

Where each finding sits inside the operational process

Domestic Dispatch Workflow

What is good: Direct coordination allows for prompt physical loading once data is clean.

What needs to be fixed: Replace unstructured WhatsApp orders with a mandatory structured intake form via the Logistics Dashboard. Connect tracking directly to Zoho Books.

Bottlenecks: Logistics Manager acts as a manual data validator for missing info (F-01). Siloed Zoho Books delays billing (F-02).

Dependencies: Sales Team (accurate order intake data), Accounts Team (timely manual billing trigger).

International Shipping & Export Workflow — F-03

What is good: Established checklist for required declarations and customs loading.

What needs to be fixed: Where carrier APIs are available, integrate them for automated vessel schedule alerts. Where APIs are unavailable, use a dashboard-connected tracking form to manage IDs/links and provide visibility across departments.

Bottlenecks: A minimum 1-week prep window is vulnerable to manual miscommunications when schedules shift without alerts.

Dependencies: Destination Agent (Handoff), Carrier (Schedule updates).

Custom Packaging & Design Workflow — F-04

What is good: Dedicated design team turns around artwork efficiently.

What needs to be fixed: Lack of upstream Sales pipeline visibility means pouches are ordered reactively. All packaging records, forecasts, and tracking must be consolidated into the Logistics Dashboard.

Bottlenecks: Vendor MOQ limitations and 15–20 day print lead time with no advance forecasting.

Dependencies: Sales (Pipeline forecast), Design (Artwork), Vendor (Printing lead time).

Section 06 — Corrective Action Plan

Recommended Solution Segment

This section outlines the integrated technology solution proposed to resolve operational bottlenecks, replacing the legacy recommendations register.

Implementation Required

Solution Modules to be Built:

1
Logistics & Supply Chain Dashboard
  • Structured Order Intake Form: A dedicated shareable intake form (accessible via link) replaces WhatsApp ordering entirely. Sales teams or clients fill it out directly; it auto-populates a central shared data layer accessible to Logistics, Accounts, Production, and R&D. Reorder data from repeat clients is auto-fetched — eliminating the need to re-confirm shipping details every time.
  • Centralised Shipment Dashboard: A single source of truth for all domestic and international shipments — replacing scattered Sheets and WhatsApp updates. CXOs can check any shipment's status independently without asking Nihal.
  • Role-Based Access Control (RBAC): Designed for future team expansion. Documents like gate passes and challans can be securely shared with Accounts, Sales, or Clients directly from the dashboard using access controls.
2
Zoho Books Integration & Document Automation
  • Zoho Books API Sync: A direct API connection from Zoho Books into the Logistics Dashboard auto-populates all dispatch tracking fields, eliminating manual double-entry across billing and shipping systems.
  • Automated Document Flow: E-gate passes, delivery challans, and all shipping documents are auto-generated and uploaded directly to the designated Google Drive folders — fully eliminating the current manual Zoho → PDF → Drive → email loop.
3
Carrier Tracking & Vessel Visibility
  • API Integration (where available): If carrier or shipping line APIs exist for current logistics partners, they will be integrated to provide automated vessel schedule alerts and real-time tracking updates directly in the dashboard.
  • Dashboard Tracking Form (fallback): Where APIs are not available, a structured dashboard form connected to the Google Sheets tracking system manages all tracking IDs and links, providing real-time visibility to all departments — eliminating the current WhatsApp-based status chase.
4
WhatsApp Monitoring & Alert Bot
  • Vendor & Client Group Intelligence: A WhatsApp bot integrated into vendor and client groups monitors incoming messages for tracking notifications, vendor payment updates, and invoicing confirmations.
  • Dashboard Alerts: The bot flags pending or upcoming invoices, outstanding vendor payments, and delivery red flags directly on the Logistics Manager's dashboard — converting unstructured WhatsApp signals into structured, actionable alerts.

What Remains the Same

  • Existing Workflows Preserved: Google Sheets, WhatsApp, Email, and Slack remain. Integration improves them — it does not replace them.
  • Vendor Experience Unchanged: External partners and vendors will not need to adopt any new platform. All integrations keep their experience identical.

Tech Stack Utilized

  • Frontend: ReactJS + TypeScript
  • APIs: Google Sheets, Drive, WhatsApp, Slack, Gmail, Zoho Books
  • Carrier APIs: Integrated where available from logistics partners
  • Host: Google Cloud Platform

Key Operational Problems Solved

  • Eliminates the 15–30 minute per-order data chasing loop from WhatsApp order intake.
  • Ends manual double-entry between Zoho Books and Google Sheets for every shipment.
  • Provides CXOs instant shipment visibility without routing through the Logistics Manager.
  • Consolidates all packaging, tracking, and document records into a single searchable, trackable database.

Future Scope of Improvement / Enhancement

  • Predictive Cost & Time Model: Based on historical order data, instantly estimate delivery time and transportation cost for new orders so Sales can give accurate commitments to customers without waiting for Logistics to manually calculate.
  • Automated Vendor Rate Optimization: Automate rate comparison from the maintained vendor rate sheet when a new order arrives, surfacing the optimal carrier automatically.

Next Steps for Team Adoption

  • Sales Team Mandate: Sales leadership must mandate exclusive use of the dashboard intake form. Any order submitted via WhatsApp without using the form is to be returned for resubmission.
  • Feedback Loop: Establish a continuous feedback loop to iterate on the solution on the ground, ensuring it genuinely adapts to the team's operational reality and achieves sustained adoption.
