Section 03

Challenges, Risks & Red Flags

1. WhatsApp-Based Order Intake (Incomplete Data)CRITICAL

Orders arrive as unstructured WhatsApp text messages with missing pin codes, GSTIN, packaging specs, and delivery instructions. Each order requires 15–30 minutes of manual follow-up.

MetricDetail
Time Waste15–30 min × 10–20 orders/day = 2.5–10 hrs/day wasted
InvestmentLow — structured digital order intake form
ROIVery High — eliminates per-order waste; data quality at source
2. Key-Person Dependency: Single Logistics ManagerCRITICAL

One logistics manager serves all segments — domestic, B2B, and international export. During peak export cycles, domestic orders are delayed and vice versa. No backup exists.

MetricDetail
Time WasteNot time waste but capacity constraint — at 2× growth, system breaks
InvestmentMedium — additional logistics personnel or segment-specific process streamlining
ROIVery High — prevents SLA breaches; avoids demurrage fees ($500–2000/incident)
3. Manual Transit TrackingMEDIUM

Post-dispatch tracking relies on manual WhatsApp updates from drivers, consuming 20–30 minutes per shipment update.

MetricDetail
Time Waste20–30 min × shipments/day
InvestmentMedium — carrier integration or tracking API
ROIMedium — automated tracking; customer visibility
4. Dual System Tracking (Zoho + Sheets)MEDIUM

Logistics maintains a parallel Google Sheets tracker alongside Zoho Books, creating duplicate data entry and reconciliation overhead.

MetricDetail
Time WasteOngoing duplicate entry; reconciliation required
InvestmentLow — consolidate into single system (Zoho)
ROIMedium — single source of truth; reduced errors
Section 04

Information Gaps & Cross-Department Leaks

⚠

Logistics ↔ All BD (Order Data Quality)

Incomplete WhatsApp orders from all BD verticals create the #1 bottleneck. No structured intake form enforces required fields before reaching Logistics.

⚠

Logistics ↔ Accounts (Invoice Dependency)

Logistics cannot dispatch without Accounts-generated invoice. Accounts cannot invoice without stock verification (phone call to Production). Triple-dependency chain creates delays.

⚠

Logistics ↔ MENA/Cocopeat (Export Capacity)

International export dispatches share the same 2-FTE team as domestic. No priority routing or segment scheduling exists.

Section 06

Proposed Solutions

Solution: Structured Digital Order Intake Form

Replace WhatsApp text orders with a digital form (Zoho Forms or custom) that enforces required fields: product, quantity, pin code, GSTIN, packaging, delivery date. BD teams fill the form — Logistics receives complete data every time.

PriorityEffortExpected Impact
P1LowEliminates 15–30 min waste per order; data quality at source

Solution: Segment-Based Dispatch Scheduling

Define dedicated time windows for export vs. domestic dispatches. During export weeks, domestic orders are batched. During domestic peaks, export prep is staged in advance.

PriorityEffortExpected Impact
P1LowPrevents cross-segment delays; structured capacity allocation

Solution: Consolidate Tracking to Zoho (Eliminate Parallel Sheets)

Migrate the parallel Google Sheets logistics tracker into Zoho Books or Zoho Inventory. Single source of truth for all shipment data.

PriorityEffortExpected Impact
P2Low–MediumEliminates dual data entry; single source of truth

Solution: Automated Carrier Tracking (Future)

Integrate carrier schedule APIs for automated vessel/truck tracking. Customers receive auto-notifications on shipment status.

PriorityEffortExpected Impact
P3MediumReduces 20–30 min/shipment manual tracking
