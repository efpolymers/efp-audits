Section 01 — Executive Summary

Department Overview — All BD Verticals

Scope, current state, and headline findings

Business Development at EF Polymers comprises 5 specialized verticals — each targeting a distinct market segment with tailored sourcing, sales, and fulfillment workflows. All verticals report to the CBDO (Ankit Jain), with the Seed vertical having a dual-report to the COO. (Puran Singh)

The review identified that despite managing a combined target of ~230 MT, the department operates entirely without a CRM system, relying on 5+ separate spreadsheets and WhatsApp. This results in extreme data fragmentation and knowledge loss risk.

Four of the seven findings are rated Critical, and they expose the department's core systemic weakness: a total lack of structural visibility. F-01 (No CRM) is the root cause of extreme data fragmentation across all 5 verticals. The remaining three criticals are operational bottlenecks unique to specific verticals: NGO receivables (F-02), Cocopeat export documentation (F-03), and B2B tertiary visibility (F-04). Phase 1 targets the immediate systemic gaps by migrating all units to Zoho CRM and decoupling the CargoX bottleneck.

Section 03 — Process Detail

Current Workflows

Analysis of current operational sequences

SEED Sales Pipeline & Demo Lifecycle

Single BD Manager runs entire pan-India 90 MT target. No CRM, no structured follow-up. 15–20% conversion rate from 25–30 active leads.

NGO/CSR Sales & Deployment Lifecycle — F-02

Follow-ups every 4–5 days from spreadsheets, relying on memory. 40–50 leads tracked in 4-tab Google Sheet.

B2B/RCM Order & Distribution Workflow — F-04

EFP supplies raw components to RCM (20 MT standing commitment). RCM blends, packages, sells through ~10,000 retail stores.
