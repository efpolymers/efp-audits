Section 01 — Executive Summary

Vertical Overview — Government

Scope, current state, and headline findings

The Government vertical facilitates government-sponsored crop trials (e.g., UK Govt Project), tracks billing compliance, gathers field usecase data, and maintains custom trial tracking software in collaboration with external developers.

Operated primarily by BD Executive Shailendra Singh (reporting to CBDO Ankit Jain), the vertical suffers from unstructured task management and high manual overhead. Daily operations, to-do lists, and field media collection run entirely through WhatsApp, leading to potential data loss and inefficient handoffs with Accounts and Logistics.

Section 03 — Process Detail

Current Workflows

Analysis of current operational sequences

Government Trials & Data Collection Workflow

What is good: Field staff consistently capture media, and custom tracking apps (Vercel) show a willingness to adopt tailored technology.

What needs to be fixed: The manual "WhatsApp-to-Drive" process creates a massive administrative burden, consuming ~25% of Shailendra's time. Task management is entirely unstructured, living in WhatsApp threads.

Bottlenecks: Lack of automated HR sync for travel/leave causes scheduling conflicts. The complete reliance on WhatsApp for daily operations prevents CBDO visibility.

Dependencies: Invoice handoffs to Accounts and Logistics are manual and prone to human error. Tool development relies on an external contractor with no formal QA/release SOPs.

Section 04 — Corrective Action Plan

Recommendations Register

Ordered by priority. Effort and impact are directional estimates for planning purposes.
IDRecommendationFindingPriorityEffortExpected Impact
R-01Centralize Task Tracking — Transition daily tasks from WhatsApp threads to a dedicated project management board (e.g., Zoho Projects or ClickUp) to give CBDO clear visibility.F-01P1LowPrevents task loss and clarifies daily priorities.

Cross-Department Dependencies

Critical Gating — Handoffs
Operations Dependency
