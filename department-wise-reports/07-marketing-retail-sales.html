Section 01 — Executive Summary

Department Overview

Scope, current state, and headline findings

The Marketing & Retail Sales team manages brand marketing, field force compliance, farmer and dealer CRM, monthly sales planning, and end-to-end order fulfillment coordination across a distributed RSM and ASM network. All real-time operational intelligence flows exclusively through WhatsApp.

The audit identified a fundamental structural dependency: the department’s entire operational data pipeline — attendance, farmer CRM, payment receipts, PO images, dispatch instructions — is routed through WhatsApp groups and manually transferred into Google Sheets by a single team member (Kamakshi Sankhla). This creates a daily bottleneck of 6–8 hours of manual data entry for one person with zero redundancy. Additionally, the marketing head (Gaurav Jain) has no real-time dashboard for sales target vs. actual visibility, relying instead on manual navigation of Zoho Books followed by manual reconciliation in a separate MIS sheet.

Section 03 — Process Detail

Current Workflows

Where each finding sits inside the operational process

Field Force Attendance & Geolocation Tracking — P-03

What is good: Location-based compliance tracking enforces field accountability across the entire distributed RSM and ASM network.

What needs to be fixed: The 3× daily cycle is entirely manual. One person (Kamakshi) reads every message, cross-checks coordinates from a reference file, and manually types each entry into Excel.

Bottlenecks: Kamakshi spends an estimated 6–8 hours daily on attendance tracking alone — consuming more than a full workday’s capacity. Any absence creates a complete data blackout.

Dependencies: Accuracy depends entirely on RSMs and ASMs sending timely, correctly located WhatsApp messages. Late or missing messages break the record.

Farmer Data Collection & CRM Workflow — P-01

What is good: Ground-level farmer intelligence — name, contact, district, village, current product, pain point — is systematically collected at every field visit.

What needs to be fixed: All data flows via informal WhatsApp messages. Kamakshi must read, interpret, and manually transcribe each record into the CRM sheet with no automated extraction.

Bottlenecks: Verification calls to confirm every meeting add a further 2–3 hours of daily manual effort on top of the CRM entry time.

Dependencies: CRM data quality depends on how precisely each ASM narrates their field visit in the WhatsApp message. Incomplete narrations result in missing records.

PO → Bill Creation → Dispatch Workflow — P-04 P-05

What is good: A clear authorization chain exists — Gaurav personally approves dispatch only after confirming payment clearance with the Accounts team, preventing unauthorized dispatch.

What needs to be fixed: Hand-written PO images create a high bill-creation error risk with no validation layer. Three separate informal communication channels (WhatsApp → email → WhatsApp) handle a single order cycle.

Bottlenecks: Gaurav must actively monitor WhatsApp for payment screenshots before he can act. Any missed message delays dispatch directly.

Dependencies: The Accounts team must confirm payment before dispatch proceeds. Delays in their confirmation cascade directly into logistics timelines.

Sales Target & MIS Reconciliation — P-02 P-06

What is good: Monthly MIS plans are highly granular — mapped state → district → distributor → salesperson → quantity target, creating full sales accountability.

What needs to be fixed: Actuals are manually extracted from Zoho Books by Gaurav personally. There is no live dashboard, no automated sync, and no real-time visibility into progress vs. target.

Bottlenecks: Zoho Books does not surface consolidated quantity data — Gaurav must compute quantities from individual invoice line items. One reconciliation cycle takes several hours.

Dependencies: Sales plan accuracy depends on Zoho Books invoice data being entered on time by the Accounts team. Any delay shifts the reconciliation cycle.

Marketing & Social Media Planning — P-07

What is good: A structured monthly content calendar is maintained, providing forward planning for post themes, farmer showcase content, and product highlights.

What needs to be fixed: There is no single analytics dashboard. Vandana checks each platform’s native insights separately — making consolidated performance reporting and data-driven content decisions impossible.

Bottlenecks: Manual posting to three platforms is time-consuming and introduces scheduling error risk. No scheduling tool is currently in use.

Dependencies: Content calendar is entirely dependent on the external vendor’s delivery timeline. Vendor delays cause posting gaps.

Section 06 — Corrective Action Plan

Recommended Solution Segment

This section outlines the integrated technology solution proposed to resolve operational bottlenecks, replacing the legacy recommendations register.

Implementation Required

Solution Modules to be Built:

1
WhatsApp AI Knowledge Hub
  • Auto-CRM Extraction: An AI bot embedded in WhatsApp groups that automatically reads field messages and populates farmer name, contact, district, village, current product, and problem directly into the CRM Google Sheet — eliminating Kamakshi’s 2–3 hrs/day of manual entry.
  • Auto-Attendance Marking: The bot detects location shares, compares coordinates to the home/field reference file, and automatically updates the attendance sheet — 3× per day, for all RSMs and ASMs, without human intervention.
  • Auto Document Upload: PO images, payment receipt photos, and visit photos are automatically routed to the correct Google Drive folder with structured naming conventions.
2
Centralized Sales Dashboard
  • Real-Time MIS Visibility: A single ReactJS dashboard connecting Google Sheets (MIS plan) and Zoho Books (actuals via API) to show live target vs. actual progress by state, district, person, and distributor.
  • Payment Status Tracker: A live view of payment status per RSM and customer — replacing the WhatsApp payment-screenshot monitoring workflow with a structured confirmation trigger.
  • CXO-Level Access: A role-based read-only executive view giving leadership real-time national and regional sales performance without requiring a Gaurav-mediated report.
3
Digital PO, Order Intake & Marketing Operations
  • Digital PO Form: A structured mobile form for RSMs/ASMs that replaces hand-written PO images. Pre-filled with customer CRM data; auto-generates a bill and routes it directly to Accounts and Logistics — removing Kamakshi’s manual bill-creation step entirely.
  • Unified Social Media Analytics: Consolidate LinkedIn, Facebook, and Instagram performance data into a single analytics view, enabling data-driven content decisions.
  • Collateral Stock Alerts: Automated minimum-stock alerts on the collateral and product material tracking sheet to prevent field material shortages during campaign periods.

What Remains the Same

  • No New Backend Database: Google Sheets and Zoho Books remain the core data sources.
  • WhatsApp as Primary Channel: The field force continues to use WhatsApp — only the extraction is automated.

Tech Stack Utilized

  • Frontend: ReactJS + TypeScript
  • APIs: WhatsApp Business API, Google Sheets, Zoho Books API
  • AI: Gemini/LLMs for message parsing & extraction
  • Host: Google Cloud Platform

Key Operational Problems Solved

  • Eliminates 6–8 hrs/day of manual WhatsApp-to-Excel data extraction by Kamakshi, freeing her for quality verification and higher-value tasks.
  • Gives Gaurav real-time target vs. actual sales visibility without manual Zoho Books navigation or MIS sheet reconciliation.
  • Removes the hand-written PO image bottleneck — digital intake auto-routes to accounts and logistics instantly.
  • Provides CXOs and leadership instant, self-service sales visibility via role-based dashboard access.
