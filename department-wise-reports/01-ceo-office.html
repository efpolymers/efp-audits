Section 01 — Executive Summary

Department Overview

Scope, current state, and headline findings

The CEO Office acts as the corporate nerve center, responsible for strategic planning, financial oversight, and international subsidiary coordination across India, Japan, the US, and EU. While daily execution is delegated to department heads, the CEO retains final approval authority on strategic policies, budgets, and all Manager+ hires.

The audit revealed significant bottlenecks stemming from disconnected operational data. Short forecasting runways (3–4 months) limit manufacturing and sales strategy, and the absence of a unified BI dashboard forces the CEO into manual, time-consuming data collection to monitor region-wise P&L. Additionally, fragmented expense approval loops in India and MENA contrast sharply with the automated Slack-based workflows successfully pioneered by the Japan subsidiary.

Section 03 — Process Detail

Current Workflows

Where each finding sits inside the operational process

Manual Administrative Approvals — P-03 P-05

What is good: CEO retains strict cost-control oversight on administrative spend.

What needs to be fixed: Unstandardized approval channels lead to lost requests and audit friction. This problem was already solved in Japan using automated Slack apps, but the solution hasn't been unified globally.

Appendix

Cross-Department Dependencies

Findings and recommendations that require action outside the CEO Office to resolve
Sales & Production
Forecast Integration
Operations Dependency
Forecasting extension requires Sales and Production to unify their projection schedules and data methodologies. Without aligned inputs from both, the runway cannot be extended beyond 3–4 months.
👤 Key Persons: Ankit Jain (Sales) / Rishabh Doshi (Production)
Critical Bottleneck
Sales demand projections and Production scheduling currently operate independently with no shared data model. The CEO cannot bridge this gap alone.
Data Transfer & Updates Needed
Sales and Production must establish a monthly forecast review cadence to validate and extend projection accuracy, feeding consolidated data directly into the CEO's dashboard.
Finance & Accounts
BI Dashboard Data
Operations Dependency
The data powering the executive BI dashboard must be accurately maintained and tagged in Zoho Books by the Accounts team. Region-wise revenue and P&L visibility depends entirely on data quality at the Finance entry point.
👤 Key Person: Shobha Shekhawat (Finance)
Critical Bottleneck
If Accounts data is incomplete or inconsistently tagged, the CEO dashboard will reflect inaccurate metrics, undermining strategic decision-making.
Data Transfer & Updates Needed
Accounts must maintain standardized tagging conventions in Zoho Books for region, client, and expense categories. Live data feeds must be configured to pull directly into the BI dashboard.
HR / Admin / Finance
Approval Workflows
Operations Dependency
Standardized Slack-based approval workflows require company-wide adoption. HR must enforce the policy that emails/Slack DMs will no longer be valid channels for expense or travel requests.
👤 Key Person: HR Manager / Dolly Mehta
Critical Bottleneck
Without HR enforcement, employees will continue to default to email and DM channels, recreating the very bottleneck the automated workflow is designed to eliminate.
Data Transfer & Updates Needed
HR must document and communicate the new approval SOP company-wide, retiring email-based submissions within 30 days. Finance must configure disbursement triggers from the Slack workflow app.
Marketing / R&D
Usecase Data Loops
Operations Dependency
The marketing team must design the intake methodology for field-level farmer feedback, while R&D must build capacity to process the incoming empirical field data for formulation improvements.
👤 Key Person: Ritu Panwar (R&D Lead)
Critical Bottleneck
Neither Marketing nor R&D currently has a structured process to receive, categorize, or act upon field-level agricultural efficacy data. The feedback loop is entirely absent.
Data Transfer & Updates Needed
A structured CRM feedback form must capture crop type, region, climate, and application method. Collected data must route directly to R&D for formulation review and to Marketing for case-study generation.

Section 06 — Corrective Action Plan

Recommended Solution Segment

This section outlines the integrated technology solution proposed to resolve operational bottlenecks, replacing the legacy recommendations register.

Implementation Required

Solution Modules to be Built:

1
Real-time Reporting & Forecast Integration
  • CEO-Level Dashboard: Connect Zoho Analytics (or equivalent BI) to pull live region-wise revenue and P&L from Zoho Books and CRM. This gives instant visibility into India revenue, MENA pipeline, and international subsidiary P&L.
  • Forecast Integration Review: Align Sales demand projections with Production scheduling to extend the forecasting model beyond the current 3-4 month window, securing supply chain stability.
2
Automated Approval Workflows
  • Standardized Workflow Apps: Roll out the automated Slack-based approval apps (currently used in Japan) across India and MENA for travel and expenses.
  • Role-based Routing: Removes the CEO bottleneck on trivial approvals by configuring approval chains—CEO approval required only for Manager+ hires and policy exceptions.
3
Usecase Data Loops
  • CRM Feedback Process: Implement structured field-feedback forms in the CRM to systematically compile ground-level farmer insights.
  • Cross-Department Routing: Route collected feedback directly to R&D for formulation improvements and to Marketing for case-study generation.

What Remains the Same

  • Core Financial Systems: Financial and sales data entry remains within Zoho Books and CRM.
  • Communication Channels: Approval workflows leverage Slack, fitting into the team's existing habits without new external software.

Tech Stack Utilized

  • Frontend: Zoho BI / Custom Dashboard
  • APIs: Zoho Suite, Slack
  • Automation: Slack Workflow Builder

Key Operational Problems Solved

  • Secures supply chain stability and enables long-term volume negotiations with raw material vendors.
  • Eliminates weekly manual data harvesting for the CEO, accelerating strategic decision making.
  • Removes the CEO bottleneck on trivial approvals while ensuring a full database audit trail for Finance.
  • Accelerates R&D formulation improvements based on actual field efficacy data.

Future Scope of Improvement / Enhancement

  • AI Predictive Modeling: In the future, predictive AI could be integrated into the forecasting model to analyze global demand trends and climate variables for strategic resource allocation.

Next Steps for Team Adoption

  • Mandatory SOP Rollout: Document and communicate the new approval SOP company-wide, ensuring all members use Slack rather than emails/DMs.
  • Executive Dashboard Review: Establish a continuous feedback loop between the CEO and IT to refine dashboard views (India, MENA, P&L, etc.) until they perfectly align with daily needs.
