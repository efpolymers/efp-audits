Section 01 — Executive Summary

Department Overview

Scope, current state, and headline findings

The CEO Office acts as the corporate nerve center, responsible for strategic planning, financial oversight, and international subsidiary coordination across India, Japan, the US, and EU. While daily execution is delegated to department heads, the CEO retains final approval authority on strategic policies, budgets, and all Manager+ hires.

The audit revealed significant bottlenecks stemming from disconnected operational data. Short forecasting runways (3–4 months) limit manufacturing and sales strategy, and the absence of a unified BI dashboard forces the CEO into manual, time-consuming data collection to monitor region-wise P&L. Additionally, fragmented expense approval loops in India and MENA contrast sharply with the automated Slack-based workflows successfully pioneered by the Japan subsidiary.

Section 02 — Risk & Control Findings

Findings Register

Ranked by risk rating. Each finding is cross-referenced to its recommendation in Section 04.
IDFindingRiskEvidence / ImpactLinked Rec.
F-01 Short-Term Forecast Visibility. Projections and demand forecasting are constrained to a 3–4 month runway. Critical Creates severe operational planning and raw material procurement risks for manufacturing and global sales execution. R-01
F-02 Data Management & Real-time Visibility Gap. No single consolidated dashboard exists for monitoring region-wise revenue, client P&L, and expense compliance. High CEO must conduct manual checks across disjointed Google Sheets and departmental silos, delaying strategic decisions. R-02
F-03 Fragmented Travel & Expense Approvals. Approvals in India and MENA are handled via unstructured email threads and Slack DMs. Medium Lack of database visibility; creates frequent administrative bottlenecks for both the CEO and the Accounts team. R-03
F-04 Siloed Ground-Level Farmer Feedback. Agricultural usecase and crop efficacy data from the ground are not systematically aggregated. Medium Missed opportunities for R&D refinement and strategic marketing messaging based on real-world farmer outcomes. R-04
F-05 Unstandardized Workflow Software. Japan subsidiary successfully uses free Slack approval apps; these have not been deployed in India/MENA. Low Unnecessary manual effort wasted on workflows that have already been solved and tested in another geography. R-03
Two of five findings are rated Critical — both relate to the CEO's ability to access real-time operational data. The short forecasting runway (F-01) and missing BI dashboard (F-02) are structurally linked: without consolidated data, forecasts cannot be extended. The two Medium findings (F-03, F-04) are self-contained quick wins. Phase 1 of the roadmap targets F-03 and F-05 as zero-cost workflow standardization before the structural data and forecasting work in Phase 2.

Section 03 — Process Detail

Current Workflows

Where each finding sits inside the operational process

High-Level Reporting Structure

The CEO acts as the direct hub for 4 domestic departments and 2 international units. The lack of a centralized dashboard (F-02) means performance metrics from each node must be harvested manually.

Manual Administrative Approvals — F-03 F-05

Unstandardized approval channels lead to lost requests and audit friction. This problem was already solved in Japan using automated Slack apps, but the solution hasn't been unified globally.

Strategic Data Consolidation — F-02

The lack of a unified BI dashboard forces the CEO into an administrative role to compile region-wise performance metrics, delaying strategic decision-making.
