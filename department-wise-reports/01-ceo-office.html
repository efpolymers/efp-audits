→

→

→

CEO grants / denies final approval

Section 04

Information Gaps & Cross-Department Leaks

⚠

CEO ↔ Sales/BD Pipeline Visibility

The CEO has no real-time access to active sales pipelines across any BD vertical. Pipeline status, deal stages, and lead ownership are invisible at the executive level, requiring manual one-to-one meetings with the CBDO.

⚠

CEO ↔ Production (Inventory & COGS)

Financial reporting is undermined by COGS margin volatility (50%–77%) caused by absent WIP valuation in the Production/Finance chain. The CEO receives unreliable cost data for strategic decisions.

⚠

CEO ↔ Field Operations (Ground Feedback)

Agricultural usecase data from ground-level farming operations does not systematically flow up to the CEO or to R&D. Feedback is trapped in WhatsApp threads of field teams.

⚠

CEO ↔ International Subsidiaries (Process Parity)

Japan subsidiary runs automated workflows through Slack apps, but these learnings and tools have not been replicated domestically. The CEO is aware of this disparity but lacks a deployment mechanism.

Section 07

Additional Optimization Suggestions

The following are not tied to specific listed problems but represent improvements that would enhance overall CEO Office effectiveness.

💡 Structured Board & Investor Reporting Pipeline

Currently, board/investor decks are compiled from verbal updates by the COO. Establishing a monthly data submission template per department (auto-populated from Zoho dashboards) would reduce deck preparation time and improve data accuracy.

Priority: P2 Effort: Low Impact: Saves 4–6 hrs/month on board prep

💡 Decision Capture Mechanism for Off-Hours

The COO reported that decisions conceived after 10 PM lack a capture channel and are prone to being forgotten. A simple voice-to-text memo workflow (Slack reminder or voice note → action item) could solve this for both CEO and COO.

Priority: P3 Effort: Minimal Impact: Prevents lost strategic ideas

💡 Quarterly Cross-Subsidiary Knowledge Exchange

The Japan subsidiary's automation success indicates a culture of process improvement. Establishing a quarterly cross-subsidiary video call focused on operational best practices would help diffuse innovation across geographies.

Priority: P3 Effort: Minimal Impact: Organizational learning; process standardization
Section 08

Phased Roadmap

The roadmap is structured by ROI impact — highest-return initiatives first. Each phase builds on the previous, progressively improving the CEO's strategic visibility and decision quality.

Phase 1 — Quick Wins (Immediate Impact)

Standardize Expense Approvals via Zoho Expense

Configure existing Zoho Expense for multi-level approval routing. Eliminate email/Slack-based approvals. Immediate time savings: ~1–2 hrs/week.

Deploy Japan Slack Workflow as Pilot

Document and replicate Japan's Slack-based approval apps in one domestic department. Zero cost, proven model.

Create Ground-Level Feedback Form

Deploy a digital feedback form for field teams to submit usecase data directly to R&D/Marketing. Closes a strategic information gap.

Where this places us: Routine approvals are automated. Ground-level agricultural feedback starts flowing to leadership. Japan's automation playbook begins domestic deployment.
Phase 2 — Operational Foundation (Strategic Visibility)

Deploy Zoho Analytics CEO Dashboard

Connect Zoho Books + CRM + Inventory into a unified analytics layer. CEO gets real-time region-wise revenue, client P&L, and expense compliance. Saves 3–5 hrs/week; enables proactive strategy.

Extend Forecast Visibility to 6–12 Months

Link CRM pipeline data and Production demand signals to build a rolling demand forecast. Reduces emergency procurement; better cash flow management.

Where this places us: CEO has real-time visibility across all departments. Strategic decisions shift from reactive quarterly cycles to proactive data-driven management. Forecast window doubles from 3–4 months to 6–12 months.
Phase 3 — Advanced Optimization

Automated Board Reporting Pipeline

Establish department-level data submission templates auto-populated from Zoho dashboards. Board decks compiled from live data rather than verbal updates. Saves 4–6 hrs/month on board prep.

Cross-Subsidiary Knowledge Exchange Program

Quarterly video calls focused on operational best practices across Japan, EU, US, and India teams. Diffuses innovation; builds organizational learning culture.

Where this places us: CEO Office operates at Level 3+ maturity. Strategic reporting is data-driven and largely automated. Organizational knowledge flows across geographies. The CEO's time is freed from operational data-gathering to focus on vision and growth.
