Section 01 — Executive Summary

Vertical Overview — Cocopeat

Scope, current state, and headline findings

The Cocopeat vertical is an export-focused business line specializing in coconut husk-based soil-less growing media (grow bags, 5kg blocks, and pellets/coins). Target markets include the UK, Europe, and the Middle East, with a D2C launch planned in Japan within 3–4 months. Products are often customized with perlite/NPK blends for specific use cases (e.g., cannabis, horticulture) and adapted for varying EC levels (High EC for Middle East landscaping; Low EC for EU growers).

Led by Mohit Kumar Gupta, alongside a team of 4, the vertical processes outbound outreach, lead nurturing, and complex international logistics workflows. The team suffers from extreme data fragmentation due to a reliance on ad-hoc spreadsheets and multiple messaging apps (WhatsApp, WeChat, LINE). Furthermore, 40% of their effort is dedicated to manual lead sourcing.

Section 03 — Process Detail

Current Workflows

Analysis of current operational sequences

Order Fulfillment & Shipment Process

What is good: The sequential export lifecycle is logically ordered — sample verification feeds negotiation, which feeds PO, which feeds production and then shipment. Clients test before committing to a full container, reducing costly surprises.

What needs to be fixed: CargoX document upload is gated behind Accounts payment clearance. Any delay in clearance confirmation pushes the upload past the mandatory 10-day arrival window, triggering demurrage fees.

Bottlenecks: For Egypt, Peru, and Spain: a 20–25 day wait for local import permits (ACID numbers) precedes CargoX eligibility, compressing the already-tight 10-day upload window further.

Dependencies: Finance/Accounts must confirm payment in real-time for BD to proceed with CargoX. Logistics must book the container and track arrival date. Any break in this chain risks demurrage.

Sourcing & Outreach Workflow

What is good: Outreach is structured with timezone awareness — China contacts at 07:00 IST, UK/US contacts after 17:00 IST. LinkedIn sequences are multi-touch (connection → case study → 3–4 follow-ups), showing process discipline.

What needs to be fixed: No CRM — all pipeline data lives in ad-hoc Google Sheets/Excel with no unified view across sourced leads, landing page inquiries, and LinkedIn responses. Inbound ad leads sit uncontacted for 6–10 hours due to timezone gaps.

Bottlenecks: Manual lead sourcing consumes ~40% of team bandwidth (Google search, AI tools, export databases, exhibition directories). Dripify LinkedIn automation subscription has lapsed, forcing manual follow-up sequences.

Dependencies: R&D must provide lab reports and technical test data to support international client qualification. Design provides packaging artwork for white-label clients. Both are ad-hoc with no SLA.

Cross-Department Dependencies

Findings and recommendations that require action outside the BD (Cocopeat) vertical to resolve.
Finance & Accounts
Critical Gating
Operations Dependency
CargoX export document upload is gated behind payment confirmation from Accounts. BD cannot proceed without explicit clearance — no API or dashboard visibility exists today.
👤 Key Person: Shobha Shekhawat (Finance)
Critical Bottleneck
Manual clearance emails create unpredictable delays. If the 10-day CargoX upload window is missed, demurrage fees become inevitable — with no automated alert to warn BD in time.
Data Transfer & Action Needed
Accounts must push a real-time payment status flag to BD and Logistics the moment payment clears — via dashboard integration or automated WhatsApp/Slack notification. BD should never need to chase Accounts manually.
R&D
Technical Support
Operations Dependency
International clients require lab test reports and technical certifications before and after sample dispatch. BD cannot qualify or close deals without R&D's turnaround on these reports.
👤 Key Person: Ritu Panwar (R&D Lead)
Critical Bottleneck
No formal SLA exists for R&D lab report delivery. QC-level queries take a minimum of 28–30 hours. BD has no visibility into queue status, stalling client qualification timelines.
Data Transfer & Action Needed
R&D must maintain a shared tracker of pending BD lab requests with SLA commitments. Completed reports must be pushed to a shared Drive folder, not delivered ad-hoc via WhatsApp.
Logistics
Fulfillment Dependency
Operations Dependency
Container booking, port arrival tracking, and customs clearance timelines must be visible to BD to accurately forecast delivery commitments to international clients.
👤 Key Person: Nihal Paliwal (Logistics)

Container arrival dates are not proactively shared with BD. The 10-day CargoX window countdown begins upon arrival — with BD often finding out too late to act within the window.
