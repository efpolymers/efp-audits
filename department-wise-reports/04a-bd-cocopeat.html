Section 01 — Executive Summary

Vertical Overview — Cocopeat

Scope, current state, and headline findings

The Cocopeat vertical is an export-focused business line specializing in coconut husk-based soil-less growing media (grow bags, 5kg blocks, and pellets/coins). Target markets include the UK, Europe, and the Middle East, with a D2C launch planned in Japan within 3–4 months. Products are often customized with perlite/NPK blends for specific use cases (e.g., cannabis, horticulture) and adapted for varying EC levels (High EC for Middle East landscaping; Low EC for EU growers).

Led by Mohit Kumar Gupta, alongside a team of 4, the vertical processes outbound outreach, lead nurturing, and complex international logistics workflows. The team suffers from extreme data fragmentation due to a reliance on ad-hoc spreadsheets and multiple messaging apps (WhatsApp, WeChat, LINE). Furthermore, 40% of their effort is dedicated to manual lead sourcing.

Section 03 — Process Detail

Current Workflows

Analysis of current operational sequences

Order Fulfillment & Shipment Process

What is good: The sequential export lifecycle is logically ordered — sample verification feeds negotiation, which feeds PO, which feeds production and then shipment. Clients test before committing to a full container, reducing costly surprises.

What needs to be fixed: CargoX document upload is gated behind Accounts payment clearance. Any delay in clearance confirmation pushes the upload past the mandatory 10-day arrival window, triggering demurrage fees.

Bottlenecks: For Egypt, Peru, and Spain: a 20–25 day wait for local import permits (ACID numbers) precedes CargoX eligibility, compressing the already-tight 10-day upload window further.

Dependencies: Finance/Accounts must confirm payment in real-time for BD to proceed with CargoX. Logistics must book the container and track arrival date. Any break in this chain risks demurrage.

Sourcing & Outreach Workflow

What is good: Outreach is structured with timezone awareness — China contacts at 07:00 IST, UK/US contacts after 17:00 IST. LinkedIn sequences are multi-touch (connection → case study → 3–4 follow-ups), showing process discipline.

What needs to be fixed: No CRM — all pipeline data lives in ad-hoc Google Sheets/Excel with no unified view across sourced leads, landing page inquiries, and LinkedIn responses. Inbound ad leads sit uncontacted for 6–10 hours due to timezone gaps.

Bottlenecks: Manual lead sourcing consumes ~40% of team bandwidth (Google search, AI tools, export databases, exhibition directories). Dripify LinkedIn automation subscription has lapsed, forcing manual follow-up sequences.

Dependencies: R&D must provide lab reports and technical test data to support international client qualification. Design provides packaging artwork for white-label clients. Both are ad-hoc with no SLA.

Section 06 — Corrective Action Plan

Recommended Solution Segment

This section outlines the integrated technology solution proposed to resolve operational bottlenecks, replacing the legacy recommendations register.

Solution Modules to be Built:

1
Cross-Department Clearance & Export Documentation
  • Real-Time Clearance Dashboard: A shared dashboard with live API integration to Accounts, showing payment clearance status instantly to BD and Logistics. CargoX upload is decoupled from manual approval chasing.
  • Regulatory Buffer Engine: Automated 25-day buffer calculation built into production and logistics timelines specifically for Egypt, Peru, and Spain orders (ACID permit approvals), preventing premature container booking.
  • Automated Alerts: WhatsApp/Slack notifications the moment payment clears or a container arrives at the destination port, protecting the 10-day CargoX upload window.
2
CRM Migration & Automated Lead Pipeline
  • Centralized CRM: Migrate lead tracking from ad-hoc spreadsheets to a unified CRM (e.g., Zoho), consolidating sourced leads, landing page inquiries, and LinkedIn responses into a single pipeline view.
  • Automated Lead Capture: An AI agent or automated WhatsApp/Email trigger that instantly qualifies and books meetings for inbound ad leads across time zones, eliminating the 6–10 hour response lag.
  • Unified Messaging: WhatsApp/WeChat/LINE threads integrated directly into the CRM, creating an auditable communication trail instead of siloed app conversations.
3
Outbound Domain Protection & Deliverability
  • Secondary Domain Infrastructure: Acquire and warm up dedicated secondary domains for Cocopeat cold outbound emails, isolating outreach traffic from the primary company domain.
  • Deliverability Monitoring: Automated inbox reputation tracking and warm-up protocols to maintain sending health and protect internal/client communications from blacklisting risk.

What Remains the Same

  • No New Backend Database: CRM sits on top of existing data flows; existing pipeline sheets feed into the new system.
  • Familiar Outreach Cadence: The team retains its structured multi-touch outreach approach; only the domain and tracking layer changes.

Tech Stack Utilized

  • CRM: Zoho (or equivalent)
  • APIs: Google Sheets, WhatsApp Business, CargoX, Gmail
  • AI: LLM-based lead qualification & response agent
  • Domain: Secondary domains with warm-up infrastructure

Key Operational Problems Solved

  • Eliminates demurrage risk by providing real-time Accounts clearance visibility and automated CargoX alerts.
  • Captures high-intent inbound leads instantly across time zones, removing the 6–10 hour response lag.
  • Unifies all lead pipelines (sourced, ad, LinkedIn) into a single auditable CRM with no manual tracking errors.
  • Protects the primary company domain from outbound blacklisting by isolating cold email traffic.

Future Scope of Improvement / Enhancement

  • Japan D2C Launch: Fully localized D2C retail operations (Amazon Japan / branded website) to capture direct consumer demand alongside the existing B2B export pipeline.
  • Predictive Lead Scoring: AI-driven scoring of outbound prospects based on historical conversion data and market signals to prioritize high-value targets.

Next Steps for Team Adoption

  • Mandatory CRM Training: All BD team members must complete onboarding on the new CRM and automated lead capture workflows before go-live.
  • Feedback Loop: Establish a continuous feedback loop to iterate on the solution on the ground, ensuring it genuinely adapts to the team's operational reality and achieves actual adoption.
