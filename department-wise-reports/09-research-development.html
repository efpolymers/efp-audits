Section 01 — Executive Summary

Department Overview

Scope, current state, and headline findings

The R&D team manages new product development, crop validation trials, regulatory registration, and international technical documentation. Operating across India, the US, EU, and Japan, the team forms the scientific backbone of EF Polymers.

The audit identified a severe key-person dependency on the Product Development Manager (Ritu Panwar), who single-handedly manages multi-stage protocol approvals and grant drafting for all regions. Additionally, research findings are fragmented across 8–10 independent Excel spreadsheets. This lack of a unified database leads to redundant testing and risks institutional knowledge loss.

Section 02 — Risk & Control Findings

Findings Register

Ranked by risk rating. Each finding is cross-referenced to its recommendation in Section 04.
IDFindingRiskEvidence / ImpactLinked Rec.
F-01 Severe Key-Person Dependency. Protocol approvals, grant writing, and technical sign-offs for all regions are bottlenecked on the PD Manager. Critical Creates a massive processing bottleneck; 15% of total time spent just on manual sign-offs. R-01
F-02 Scattered Scientific Data. Research findings are fragmented across 8–10 disconnected spreadsheets. Critical Causes redundant testing, data loss, and prevents cross-referencing past trials. R-02
F-03 Aborted Project Management Tooling. Project tracking reverted to manual sheets after PM tools (Asana) were abandoned. Medium Tasks fall through the cracks without automated reminders or clear ownership tracking. R-03
F-04 Task Ambiguity and Overload. Lack of formal load balancing for secondary support roles has led to task imbalances. Low Inefficient utilization of newly onboarded executives and interns. R-04
The two Critical findings jeopardize the company's core scientific IP and speed-to-market. F-01 (Key-Person Dependency) creates an unsustainable bottleneck where global approvals stall waiting for a single manager. F-02 (Scattered Data) is a major structural risk—legacy trial data across 8-10 Excel sheets leads to redundant re-testing and knowledge loss. Phase 1 targets both by enforcing an approval delegation matrix and migrating all disparate sheets into a unified Power BI relational database.

Section 04 — Corrective Action Plan

Recommendations Register

Ordered by priority. Effort and impact are directional estimates for planning purposes.
IDRecommendationFindingPriorityEffortExpected Impact
R-01 Approval Delegation Framework — Draft clear rubrics allowing the PD Manager to delegate stage 1 & 2 protocol approvals to senior executives, reserving her time for final sign-off. F-01 P1 Medium De-bottlenecks trial deployment and empowers mid-level scientific staff.
R-02 Unified Master R&D Database — Consolidate the 8-10 tracking sheets into a unified Power BI database or Zoho Creator app with relational search. F-02 P1 High Protects core scientific IP; prevents repeat trials due to lost legacy data.
R-03 Low-Friction PM Tool Reboot — Reintroduce a simplified project management tool (e.g., Trello or Monday) for task assignment without heavy administrative bloat. F-03 P2 Low Restores task accountability and automated deadline reminders.
R-04 Role Standardization — Formally map out specific ownership domains for the new support hires to balance the reporting and tracking workload. F-04 P2 Low Improves execution speed across the 18-20 peak active projects.
