Section 01 — Executive Summary

Department Overview

Scope, current state, and headline findings

The R&D team manages new product development, crop validation trials, regulatory registration, and international technical documentation. Operating across India, the US, EU, and Japan, the team forms the scientific backbone of EF Polymer Private Limited.

The audit identified a severe key-person dependency on the Product Development Manager (Ritu Panwar), who single-handedly manages multi-stage protocol approvals and grant drafting for all regions. Additionally, research findings are fragmented across 8–10 independent Excel spreadsheets. This lack of a unified database leads to redundant testing and risks institutional knowledge loss.

Section 03 — Process Detail

Current Workflows

Where each finding sits inside the operational process

Product Validation & Trial Lifecycle

What is good: The sequence from formulation to field deployment is logically ordered and well-defined across teams.

What needs to be fixed: Validation is R&D's largest cost center. Untracked findings lead to repeated trials and inflated validation budgets. Data is logged manually into scattered sheets.

Bottlenecks: Retrieving historical data from unlinked Google Drive trackers risks data loss and prevents learning from past trials.

Dependencies: 3-department handoff — Production assigns the lot number → QC runs the 24-hr test and issues a QC report → R&D logs the result. Any break here derails the trial.

B2B Proposal & Protocol Drafting — P-01

What is good: Comprehensive 3-step technical and budget screening prevents unviable trials from reaching the field.

What needs to be fixed: All international proposals and scientific grants bottleneck on a single person (PD Manager). Requires 2-3 manual drafts per proposal with no standardized templates.

Bottlenecks: 3-step auditing process is non-delegable and directly inflates proposal timelines. Multi-round reviews can take up to 15-20 days.

Dependencies: BD and Sales team are entirely reliant on Ritu Panwar for technical pitching materials and final sign-offs.

Protocol Approval Gate — P-01 P-04

What is good: Gate system is well-defined and ensures rigorous technical assessment of trial requests.

What needs to be fixed: The compounded approval load of 18-20 active projects in peak season is entirely centralized on one person.

Bottlenecks: Non-delegable gate. All 3 steps are personally executed by the PD Manager, causing significant delays.

Dependencies: BD and Sales teams depend entirely on Ritu's bandwidth to move any trial forward.

Multi-Tracker Data Flow & Consolidation — P-02 P-03

What is good: Department maintains detailed, real-time trackers for every region and function.

What needs to be fixed: Data is siloed across 8-10 disconnected Google Sheets. There is no automated aggregation or cross-referencing between trackers.

Bottlenecks: Ritu is the sole consolidation point. The manual aggregation of these sheets delays project portfolio visibility.

Dependencies: R&D is dependent on timely updates from regional staff and Production/QC to keep trackers accurate.

Sales & BD → R&D Query Resolution — P-07

What is good: Query triage is naturally tiered by complexity and required effort.

What needs to be fixed: No self-serve knowledge base for Sales or BD. All query tiers are routed directly to R&D team members, disrupting focus time.

Bottlenecks: Critical-tier queries (QC-level and Field POC) have response times measured in days to months, stalling Sales closures.

Dependencies: Sales is entirely dependent on R&D for even basic known-crop data retrieval.

Monthly Reporting & BOD Aggregation — P-08

What is good: Consolidated monthly updates provide full visibility to the Board and CXOs.

What needs to be fixed: The reporting cycle runs manually with no automated reminders, no standardized aggregation template, and no pre-built BOD format.

Bottlenecks: Ritu personally coordinates across at least 3 time zones to collect data before she can begin compiling.

Dependencies: Aggregation depends completely on agronomists sending data on time; delays cascade down the line.

Section 06 — Corrective Action Plan

Recommended Solution Segment

This section outlines the integrated technology solution proposed to resolve operational bottlenecks, replacing the legacy recommendations register.

Implementation Required

Solution Modules to be Built:

1
R&D Frontend Dashboard
  • Real-Time Data Sync: The dashboard links directly to your existing Google Sheets. If someone edits a Google Sheet, the dashboard updates instantly. If you make a change on the dashboard, it automatically saves back to the Google Sheet, meaning no data is ever out of sync.
  • Centralised Approval System: A dedicated space where the R&D Head can instantly see all pending requests. You can request, review, approve, or reject trials and documents with a single click, eliminating delays caused by lost emails or messages.
  • Workload & Capacity Tracking: A clear, visual breakdown of what every team member is currently working on. It helps the manager foresee delays, balance task distribution, and track exactly how much time is being spent on each project or trial.
2
AI & Knowledge Systems
  • Proposal Drafting Engine: A custom AI assistant built specifically for your team that reads existing past proposals and templates securely stored on Google Drive. It automatically drafts new proposals in the exact required format, saving hours of manual writing. Crucially, this system will live directly inside the new R&D dashboard so the team doesn't have to jump between multiple tools.
  • Single Source of Truth (SSOT) Chatbot: We will upgrade the existing chatbot so it has access to the entire R&D knowledge base. Sales and BD teams can ask it questions and get instant, accurate answers without interrupting the R&D team's actual work.
3
Automated Communications & Operations
  • Reporting Reminders: Automated workflows via API (WhatsApp for internal, Slack for international, Gmail for external partners).
  • Unified OS integration: Integrates into the larger EF Polymer operating system, giving CXOs role-based, one-click visibility.

What Remains the Same

  • No New Backend Database: Google Sheets and Drive remain the core sources.
  • Familiar Workflows: The team can still update sheets manually without being forced to change underlying habits.

Tech Stack Utilized

  • Frontend: ReactJS + TypeScript
  • APIs: Google Sheets, Drive, WhatsApp, Slack, Gmail
  • AI: Gemini/LLMs for RAG & Drafting
  • Host: Google Cloud Platform

Key Operational Problems Solved

  • Eliminates manual chasing of data and broken information flows across time zones.
  • Resolves the massive documentation and proposal drafting bottleneck (saving significant PD Manager time).
  • Centralizes tracking without forcing the team onto a new, complex PM tool.
  • Provides CXOs and department heads instant visibility via a unified approval flow.

Future Scope of Improvement / Enhancement

  • AI Operations Agent: In the future, an AI agent could be added to actively monitor task/trial data, identify bottlenecks, flag delays, and analyze team communications for proactive management. This is not needed right now but is a planned future enhancement.

Next Steps for Team Adoption

  • Mandatory Training: Team adoption and training sessions must be made compulsory to ensure all team members understand how to use the dashboard and AI tools.
  • Feedback Loop: Establish a continuous feedback loop to iterate on the solution on the ground, ensuring it genuinely adapts to the team's operational reality and achieves actual adoption.
