Section 01 — Executive Summary

Department Overview

Scope, current state, and headline findings

The Human Resources department, led by Dolly Mehta, governs the entire employee lifecycle—from Q4 strategic hiring to performance appraisals, payroll, and eventual offboarding.

The audit revealed a heavily manual, disconnected ecosystem. Recruitment relies on isolated Google Sheets, creating a complete blind spot for leadership regarding pipeline health. Once hired, employees are not automatically provisioned across Zoho apps, forcing redundant data entry. Furthermore, performance appraisals remain tied to infrequent, subjective annual discussions rather than being linked to real-time, monthly KPI tracking.

Section 02 — Risk & Control Findings

Findings Register

Ranked by risk rating. Each finding is cross-referenced to its recommendation in Section 04.
IDFindingRiskEvidence / ImpactLinked Rec.
F-01 Lack of Unified Applicant Tracking (ATS). The hiring pipeline relies entirely on disconnected Google Sheets and Forms. Critical Creates a visibility gap for leadership; resumes and interview notes are easily lost. R-01
F-02 Siloed Onboarding & Process Automation. New hires are not automatically provisioned across company platforms. Critical Requires manual, error-prone data entry into Zoho People, L&D modules, and payroll. R-02
F-03 Manual Approval Bottlenecks. Offer letter validations and resource requests are processed via unstructured email threads. Medium Causes delays in capturing top talent while waiting for inbox responses from the COO. R-04
F-04 Subjective and Infrequent Appraisals. Performance management is tied to annual discussions rather than live KPIs. Medium Fails to address underperformance quickly; disconnects sales realities from HR scoring. R-03
F-05 Ad-hoc L&D Infrastructure. Training requests and skills tracking are handled without formal systems. Low The company lacks a unified "skills directory" to target development needs accurately. R-05
The two Critical findings expose EF Polymers to systemic data loss and severe administrative bloat during periods of high growth. F-01 (No ATS) forces HR to track 60-80 candidates across fragile Google Sheets, creating a complete blind spot for leadership. F-02 (Manual Onboarding) means every new hire triggers a wave of redundant, manual profile creation across Zoho platforms. Phase 1 targets this by deploying Zoho Recruit to centralize applicant tracking and automating the onboarding handoff to Zoho People.

Section 04 — Corrective Action Plan

Recommendations Register

Ordered by priority. Effort and impact are directional estimates for planning purposes.
IDRecommendationFindingPriorityEffortExpected Impact
R-01 Zoho Recruit (ATS) Rollout — Implement an Applicant Tracking System to unify candidate sourcing, resume parsing, and pipeline dashboards for leadership. F-01 P1 High Provides total visibility into the 60-80 candidate pipeline and prevents data loss.
R-02 Automated Onboarding Workflows — Configure Zoho People to automatically provision profiles, assign KPIs, and trigger document checks the moment an offer is accepted. F-02 P1 Medium Eliminates manual profile building and sets immediate expectations for new hires.
R-03 Monthly KPI-Linked PMS — Transition from annual subjective reviews to a monthly Zoho PMS cadence mapped directly to operational and sales targets. F-04 P2 High Aligns HR performance tracking with actual business realities and corrects behavior faster.
R-04 Digital Approval Routing — Replace email-based offer and policy approvals with structured, one-click Zoho approval flows. F-03 P2 Low Accelerates final hiring decisions and standardizes the audit trail.
R-05 Skills Inventory Database — Build a master L&D directory mapping "who knows what," allowing targeted training and cross-functional deployment. F-05 P3 Medium Transforms L&D from ad-hoc reaction to strategic talent development.
