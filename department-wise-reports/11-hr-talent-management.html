Section 01 — Executive Summary

Department Overview

Scope, current state, and headline findings

The Human Resources department comprises five team members across three verticals — Hiring, Admin, and HR/PMS — governing the entire employee lifecycle from strategic recruitment and onboarding to performance evaluation, payroll, and offboarding.

The audit revealed a heavily manual, disconnected ecosystem. Recruitment sourcing spans six+ platforms (LinkedIn, Naukri, Indeed, Apollo, SalesIQ, and external GPT/Perplexity for contact scraping) with no unified tracking layer, creating a complete blind spot for leadership. Performance management relies on scattered Google Sheets for KPI/KRA tracking, preventing automation of incentives and forcing subjective annual appraisals. Admin support tickets are raised on WhatsApp and email with zero live tracking. Policy queries — especially from the BD and Sales teams regarding travel, stays, and reimbursements — consume significant HR bandwidth with no self-serve mechanism.

Section 03 — Process Detail

Current Workflows

Where each finding sits inside the operational process

Recruitment Sourcing & Screening Cycle — H-01 H-02

What is good: Multi-platform sourcing ensures wide candidate reach. CXO involvement in priority assignment ensures strategic alignment for hiring.

What needs to be fixed: No unified platform to track all candidates. HR must switch between 6+ platforms to check status. Contact details are scraped using external GPT/Perplexity — no integrated scraping layer. No AI pre-screening — every profile is manually reviewed at 10 minutes each.

Bottlenecks: 30–40 profiles screened per day manually. 22–25 candidates scraped per day. Average closing rate of 30–45 days per opening. Previously screened candidates from unclosed roles cannot be re-tracked for similar future openings.

Dependencies: Department heads must provide JDs, salary, working hours, and specific requirements. CXOs assign priority order. External platforms control candidate flow.

Interview & Selection Process — H-01 H-06

What is good: Structured cultural fit checklist exists. 0–5 rating system by all stakeholders (technical heads, department heads, HR). 3–4 round structure with assignment add-ons is well-defined.

What needs to be fixed: All ratings tracked on Google Sheets with no centralized portal. Hiring rounds should be customizable per role but currently follow a rigid template. Offer letters and NDAs are manually customized per candidate with no AI-assisted drafting.

Bottlenecks: Manual compilation of 0–5 ratings from multiple stakeholders. Email-based offer letter approval with no audit trail. No e-signature workflow.

Dependencies: HODs/TLs must conduct technical interviews and submit ratings. CXOs must conduct final interviews for HOD+ roles. No system enforces SLA on any step.

HR Audit Report · v1.0Page 5

Onboarding & Document Management — H-06

What is good: Onboarding process is documented on Google Docs. Documents are templatized at a base level. All files stored on Google Drive.

What needs to be fixed: No automated reminders to internal teams or candidates for upcoming joinings. No dashboard reminder for HR. Offer letters and NDAs manually customized per candidate discussion. No e-signature platform. No document directory — files scattered on Drive with no single source of truth for retrieval.

Bottlenecks: When hiring happens mid-month with joining at month start, manual coordination creates gaps. Document retrieval for any team member requires manual Drive searching. No tracking of which documents are signed vs pending.

Dependencies: HR executive manages onboarding. IT must provision accounts. Department heads must conduct induction. No system orchestrates these handoffs.

Performance Management System (PMS) — H-04

What is good: KPI/KRA framework exists. Multi-level review process (Manager → HR → CXO) is structured. HOD-level reviews involve CXOs directly.

What needs to be fixed: Not on software — entirely on Google Sheets. Cannot automate incentive calculations. Annual frequency delays feedback loops. Appraisals are subjective, decoupled from real-time operational data (sales targets, QC metrics).

Bottlenecks: Manual compilation of KPI/KRA data from multiple sheets. No link between performance scores and incentive calculation. Probation reviews (3-month) are manual with no automated trigger.

Dependencies: Reporting managers must verify KPI/KRA data. CXOs must review HOD performance. Finance must reconcile incentive payouts. No system connects these stakeholders automatically.

Admin Support & Policy Query Resolution — H-03 H-05

What is good: WhatsApp provides a quick, familiar communication channel for the ground team. Budget approval slabs are defined in policy documents.

What needs to be fixed: No ticket tracking system — admin has no live visibility of open, closed, or pending tickets. Budget approvals are lost in email threads with no audit trail. Policy queries from BD/Sales (travel, stays, reimbursements) consume HR bandwidth with no self-serve mechanism. No system to flag when a query falls outside existing policy coverage.

Bottlenecks: Issues get lost in WhatsApp message threads. HR time is consumed answering repetitive policy questions. Budget approval routing is manual — no slab-based auto-routing despite slabs being defined in policy.

Dependencies: Admin team resolves tickets manually. HR answers all policy queries. CXOs approve budgets via email. No system connects these workflows or provides visibility.

Field Attendance & Temp Employee Tracking — H-09

What is good: WhatsApp location sharing provides a basic attendance signal from field staff without requiring a new app.

What needs to be fixed: Previous live-tracking software failed. Temp employees are excluded from Zoho People to avoid per-head billing costs. HR manually enters WhatsApp location data into Google Sheets. No live attendance dashboard for field teams.

Bottlenecks: Manual data entry from WhatsApp to Sheets is error-prone and time-consuming. No real-time visibility into field team attendance. Monthly reconciliation is manual.

Dependencies: Field staff must share locations on WhatsApp. HR must manually transcribe. No automated pipeline exists between WhatsApp location shares and the attendance sheet.

Section 06 — Corrective Action Plan

Recommended Solution Segment

This section outlines the integrated technology solution proposed to resolve operational bottlenecks, following the extend-don't-replace principle.

Implementation Required

Solution Modules to be Built:

1
Unified Recruitment Dashboard & Onboarding Engine
  • Centralized ATS Layer: Connects to LinkedIn, Naukri, Indeed, Apollo, and SalesIQ via APIs. HR continues using these platforms — the dashboard sits on top as a unified tracking layer. No platform switching required.
  • AI Pre-Screening Agent: A Gemini-powered agent that matches candidate profiles against JD parameters and prerequisite questions. Replaces the manual 10-minute-per-profile screening with automated first-layer filtering.
  • Candidate Re-Engagement Database: Previously screened candidates from unclosed roles are indexed. When a similar role opens, the system suggests past candidates who may be available — eliminating repeat sourcing effort.
  • Employee Referral Tracking: Formal module within the dashboard replacing WhatsApp/email/Sheets tracking. Referrals link directly to candidate profiles.
  • Customizable Hiring Rounds: Digitizes the 0–5 rating system and cultural fit checklist. Rounds are configurable per role (3–4 rounds with assignment add-ons). HODs/TLs and CXOs submit ratings directly in the portal.
  • Onboarding Automation: Automated WhatsApp/email reminders to internal teams and candidates before joining dates. E-signature workflow for offer letters and NDAs. AI-assisted document customization based on role and discussion points. Document directory built over Google Drive as a single source of truth.
2
AI Policy Assistant & Knowledge System
  • WhatsApp Policy Chatbot: A Gemini-powered RAG chatbot that reads all policy documents from Google Drive. Team members — especially BD/Sales — ask questions on WhatsApp (where they already are) about travel, stays, reimbursements, and any policy. Answers are instant and grounded in company policy.
  • Slack Integration for International Team: Same AI assistant available on Slack for the international team, ensuring no channel fragmentation.
  • Policy Gap Detection: When a query falls outside existing policy coverage, the system flags it to HR with a recommendation to update policies. Closes the feedback loop between ground-team questions and policy evolution.
  • Anonymous Feedback & Grievance Form: Integrated feedback mechanism where team members can raise concerns anonymously or visible only to specific HR members. Replaces the current hesitancy in direct communication.
3
Admin Ticketing & Engagement Portal
  • WhatsApp-Native Ticket Ingestion: An AI agent reads incoming WhatsApp messages from team members and auto-creates tracked tickets. The ground team continues raising issues on WhatsApp — no behavior change required. Complexity is absorbed by the AI layer, not pushed onto employees.
  • Live Ticket Dashboard: Real-time visibility of open, closed, and pending tickets. Admin team and CXOs can see status, assignee, and resolution time without digging through WhatsApp threads.
  • Slab-Based Budget Approval Routing: Digitizes the fixed budget slabs from policy. Requests auto-route to HR, HOD, or CXO based on amount — no more email chains or lost approvals.
  • Employee Engagement Polling: Saturday team activity polling, confirmation tracking, and idea collection — all within WhatsApp or the dashboard. Replaces ad-hoc WhatsApp group discussions.
  • Field Attendance AI Agent: Reads WhatsApp location shares from field/temp employees and auto-updates the attendance Google Sheet. No manual transcription. No new app for field staff. Extends the existing WhatsApp behavior they already follow.
4
Performance Management & L&D System
  • Monthly KPI/KRA Dashboard: Extends the existing Google Sheets with a dashboard layer. Managers continue updating Sheets — the dashboard provides visibility, analytics, and automated compilation. No data migration required.
  • 0–5 Rating Digitization: Interview and performance ratings move from Sheets to the dashboard. Manager → HR → CXO workflow is automated with notifications and SLA tracking.
  • Incentive Automation: Performance scores link directly to incentive calculation rules. Probation reviews (3-month) trigger automatically.
  • Skills Matrix Directory: Centralized "who knows what" directory mapping internal competencies. Identifies training gaps and available expertise.
  • L&D Request Portal & LMS: Structured training request portal replacing verbal collection. Induction prerequisite assets managed digitally. Training efficacy tracked with standardized assessment forms.

What Remains the Same

  • No New Backend Database: Google Sheets and Drive remain the core data sources. Zoho People & Payroll remain for on-payroll employees.
  • Sourcing Platforms Stay: LinkedIn, Naukri, Indeed, Apollo, SalesIQ remain in use — the dashboard connects on top.
  • WhatsApp Stays Primary: Ground team communication doesn't change. AI agents read and update from WhatsApp directly.

Tech Stack Utilized

  • Frontend: ReactJS + TypeScript
  • Backend: Node.js
  • APIs: Google Sheets, Google Drive, Zoho People, WhatsApp Business, Slack, LinkedIn, Apollo/Naukri
  • AI: Gemini LLM + LangGraph (agentic workflows for WhatsApp → ticket ingestion, location → attendance update, query → policy answer)
  • Host: Google Cloud Platform

Key Operational Problems Solved

  • Eliminates multi-platform sourcing chaos with a unified ATS layer — no platform switching, no manual scraping.
  • Resolves the 10-minute-per-profile screening bottleneck with AI pre-screening against JD parameters.
  • Provides live admin ticket visibility and automated slab-based budget approval routing — replacing WhatsApp/email chaos.
  • Enables self-serve policy queries via WhatsApp chatbot — freeing HR from repetitive question-answering.
  • Automates onboarding reminders, e-signatures, and document directory — replacing manual Google Docs processes.
  • Links performance scores to incentive calculations — replacing subjective annual appraisals with data-driven monthly reviews.
  • Auto-syncs field attendance from WhatsApp location shares to Google Sheets — no manual transcription, no new app for field staff.

Future Scope of Improvement / Enhancement

  • Vendor Management Module: A shared module for HR and Accounts to track external vendors. Identified as low priority by the client but planned for future integration.
  • Predictive Attrition Analytics: An AI layer that reads PMS data, engagement survey results, and attendance patterns to flag flight-risk employees before resignation.
  • AI Recruitment Operations Agent: An agentic workflow that proactively sources, pre-screens, and shortlists candidates when a new requisition is opened — reducing HR involvement to final review only.

Next Steps for Team Adoption

  • Mandatory Training: Adoption sessions must be compulsory for all 5 HR team members, with role-specific walkthroughs for each vertical (Hiring, Admin, PMS).
  • Phased Rollout: Start with the Admin Ticketing module (quickest behavior change — team already on WhatsApp) before introducing the Recruitment Dashboard (largest workflow shift).
  • Feedback Loop: Establish a continuous feedback mechanism so the solution iterates based on ground-team reality. The AI policy assistant's gap-detection feature creates a natural feedback loop for policy evolution.
