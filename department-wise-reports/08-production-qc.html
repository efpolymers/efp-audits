Section 01 — Executive Summary

Department Overview

Scope, current state, and headline findings

The Production department manages raw material procurement, manufacturing, quality control, packaging, and dispatch. Led by Rishabh Doshi, the team (3 core FTE + 4 recent hires; Recently expanded) operates on a "Produce to Stock" model, maintaining minimum inventory thresholds of semi-finished and finished goods to fulfill sudden sales demands across domestic and international markets.

The audit identified severe data fragmentation across multiple disconnected Google Sheets. The team previously attempted to implement a custom Zoho tool, but adoption failed, forcing a reversion to manual spreadsheets. Consequently, real-time inventory visibility is non-existent for CXOs, Accounts, and Sales. Furthermore, custom packaging designs and vendor MOQs create frequent production bottlenecks and dead stock, while last-minute sales requests force unplanned labor overtime, inflating COGS without automated tracking.

Section 03 — Process Detail

Current Workflows

Where each finding sits inside the operational process

Core Fulfillment & Inventory Lifecycle

What is good: The "Produce to Stock" model using Semi-Finished Goods (SFG) allows rapid fulfillment once sales orders arrive.

What needs to be fixed: Inventory levels across all stages are tracked in disconnected Google Sheets. Real-time visibility is lost.

Bottlenecks: Last-minute sales triggers often force labor overtime, adding uncalculated COGS to the product cycle.

Dependencies: Production relies on BD/Sales for forecasting, which is currently erratic, preventing proactive inventory maintenance.

Custom Packaging Design & MOQ Friction — P-02 P-05

What is good: There is a structured loop from design to client approval before production begins.

What needs to be fixed: The entire workflow runs on WhatsApp/Email. Custom packaging requests frequently encounter severe MOQ mismatches (e.g., vendor MOQ of 16,000 pouches vs. client order of only 5,000 pouches), forcing the company to procure excess packaging and creating up to a 68% surplus of unusable dead stock pouches.

Bottlenecks: Manual revisions and physical vendor outreach for every custom design.

Dependencies: Production is entirely dependent on the BD team and Marketing designer to finalize and approve designs before ordering packaging.

Production Audit Report · v1.0Page 5

Multi-Sheet Inventory Data Flow & CXO Reporting — P-01 P-03

What is good: The department maintains detailed trackers for every operational stage and vendor.

What needs to be fixed: Data is siloed across 5+ disconnected Google Sheets. There is no automated aggregation.

Bottlenecks: Rishabh is the sole consolidation point. Manual aggregation delays executive visibility for Founder & CEO (Narayan Lal Gurjar) and Co-Founder & COO (Puran Singh Rajput) until month-end.

Dependencies: Accounts and Sales are dependent on Production's manual exports to calculate COGS and dispatch status.

Quality Control & Cross-Verification Flow — P-04

What is good: Three distinct layers of checking (QC, QA, QI) ensure batch integrity and product quality across raw, in-process, and packaging stages.

What needs to be fixed: Cross-verification is done manually across multiple team members with no single platform to maintain a record of who verified what.

Bottlenecks: Critical-tier checks have no automated audit trail. Documents are scattered across Drive and Sheets.

Dependencies: Dispatch is entirely dependent on manual sign-offs, risking compliance during external audits.

Section 06 — Corrective Action Plan

Recommended Solution Segment

This section outlines the integrated technology solution proposed to resolve operational bottlenecks, replacing the legacy recommendations register.

Implementation Required

Solution Modules to be Built:

1
Unified Inventory Dashboard
  • Real-Time Data Sync: The dashboard links directly to your existing Google Sheets. If someone edits a Google Sheet, the dashboard updates instantly. If you make a change on the dashboard, it automatically saves back to the Google Sheet, meaning no data is ever out of sync.
  • Stock & WIP Visibility: A clear, visual breakdown of raw materials, semi-finished goods (SFG), and fully processed goods ready to dispatch. Red alerts will flag expiring materials, wastage, or minimum inventory breaches.
  • CXO & Accounts Access: Role-based visibility allows CXOs to view production metrics in real-time and Accounts to access dispatch/invoice data without waiting for month-end reports.
2
AI & Smart Procurement Engine
  • Vendor Directory & MOQ Optimizer: Centralizes the scattered vendor sheets into a searchable directory. When a custom order comes in, AI automatically matches the quantity to vendor MOQs, suggesting the best pricing or identifying if orders should be combined.
  • Overtime & COGS Calculator: An automated tool that calculates labor overtime triggered by last-minute sales and directly syncs this additional cost to the Accounts team for accurate COGS calculation.
3
Automated Comms & Design Approvals
  • WhatsApp Agent Integration: Keeps the team on their preferred channel. Automated alerts for low inventory, QC verification requests, and dispatch approvals.
  • Unified Design Link: A single platform link for BD, the designer, and the client to approve custom packaging. Revisions auto-notify Production, who then trigger the vendor dispatch with one click.
  • Vendor Onboarding Forms: Self-serve forms for new vendors to upload GST/PAN/docs, which automatically route to the Accounts team for verification.

What Remains the Same

  • No New Backend Database: Google Sheets and Drive remain the core sources.
  • Familiar Workflows: The team can still update sheets manually without being forced to change underlying habits.

Tech Stack Utilized

  • Frontend: ReactJS + TypeScript
  • APIs: Google Sheets, Drive, WhatsApp Business
  • AI: Gemini/LLMs for Forecasting & Vendor Matching
  • Host: Google Cloud Platform

Key Operational Problems Solved

  • Eliminates manual end-of-month reporting and provides real-time stock visibility.
  • Resolves custom packaging MOQ bottlenecks by intelligently matching orders to vendors.
  • Automates overtime calculation, ensuring accurate COGS accounting.
  • Provides a single traceable link for design approvals and QC verifications.

Future Scope of Improvement / Enhancement

  • AI Sales Forecasting Agent: In the future, an AI agent could actively analyze historical seasonal sales data and current BD pipelines to generate highly accurate 90-day production forecasts, proactively maintaining minimum inventory levels.

Next Steps for Team Adoption

  • Mandatory Training: Team adoption and training sessions must be made compulsory to ensure all team members understand how to use the dashboard and AI tools.
  • Feedback Loop: Establish a continuous feedback loop to iterate on the solution on the ground, ensuring it genuinely adapts to the team's operational reality and achieves actual adoption.
