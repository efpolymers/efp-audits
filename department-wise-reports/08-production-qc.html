Section 01 — Executive Summary

Department Overview

Scope, current state, and headline findings

The Production department, led by Rishabh Doshi, manages chemical granulation, quality inspection, and warehouse fulfillment across the Udaipur and Coimbatore plants. The core strategy is "Produce to Stock, Stock to Pack," yielding varied granular sizes across multiple custom SKUs.

The audit uncovered major financial exposure tied to custom B2B packaging Minimum Order Quantities (MOQs). When Sales requests a custom 5,000-unit print run, vendor MOQs force Production to purchase 16,000 pouches, storing 11,000 surplus bags as dead inventory. Furthermore, the custom Zoho Warehouse Management System (WMS) is structurally siloed from the Accounts Zoho Books instance, forcing manual stock reporting.

Section 02 — Risk & Control Findings

Findings Register

Ranked by risk rating. Each finding is cross-referenced to its recommendation in Section 04.
IDFindingRiskEvidence / ImpactLinked Rec.
F-01 Surplus Custom Packaging Risk. Custom pouch MOQs result in major warehouse surplus and direct write-off exposure if client branding updates. Critical Orders of 5,000 force 16,000 pouch print runs, yielding 68% immediate dead stock. R-01
F-02 Siloed Zoho WMS Database. The custom Zoho WMS is disconnected from the main Zoho Books ERP. Critical Requires manual Excel sheet exports for Accounts to calculate COGS margins. R-02
F-03 Absence of Sales Packaging Forecasts. B2B Sales requests custom specs at order intake without providing lead-time buffers. Medium Creates sudden production bottlenecks and rushed, premium-priced printer negotiations. R-03
F-04 Finished Goods Expiry Exposure. Premium pricing prevents discounted liquidation of aging finished goods. Low Risk of outright write-offs for 3-year old export inventory. R-04
F-05 Manual Vendor Registration Process. Vendor onboarding (GST, PAN, bank details) relies on disconnected forms and emails, forcing duplicate manual entry. Medium Creates data silos between Production's vendor list and Accounts' master ledger. R-05
F-06 Undocumented Practical Training. New floor hires receive verbal "practical training," lacking formalized, trackable digital records of SOP completion. Low Increases compliance risk during audits if safety/process training cannot be verified. R-06
The two Critical findings highlight severe structural and financial risks. F-01 (Surplus Packaging) exposes the company to massive direct write-offs, with 68% of custom print runs turning into dead stock due to vendor MOQs. F-02 (Siloed WMS) breaks the core financial data flow, forcing manual Excel exports for basic COGS valuation. Phase 1 targets the immediate cash bleed by standardizing B2B packaging and enforcing forecasting, while Phase 2 bridges the critical software gap.

Section 04 — Corrective Action Plan

Recommendations Register

Ordered by priority. Effort and impact are directional estimates for planning purposes.
IDRecommendationFindingPriorityEffortExpected Impact
R-01 Standardized B2B Packaging Templates — Shift custom B2B fulfillment to blank, standardized pouches utilized across all clients, personalized via on-demand custom sticker labels. F-01 P1 High Bypasses 16,000 MOQ print constraints; eliminates custom pouch surplus write-offs.
R-02 WMS / ERP Integration — Develop API bridges or migrate the custom Zoho WMS natively into Zoho Inventory/Books to provide real-time valuation. F-02 P1 High Secures automated COGS margins for Finance without weekly manual sheet exports.
R-03 Rolling 90-Day Forecasts — Enforce a 90-day rolling sales forecast from the BD team for any custom branding orders requiring external printing. F-03 P2 Medium Restores lead-time buffers, reducing expedited print costs and production jams.
R-04 Automated Expiry Alerts — Configure WMS alerts at the 2.5-year mark for finished goods to prompt aggressive export or liquidation strategies before total expiration. F-04 P3 Low Reduces final write-off exposure on slow-moving inventory.
R-05 Centralized Vendor Portal — Digitize vendor registration via a self-serve portal (e.g., Zoho Creator) that feeds master data directly into Zoho Books. F-05 P2 Medium Eliminates manual transcription and ensures data parity between Production and Accounts.
R-06 Formalize SOP Training Records — Implement a digital sign-off system (e.g., Zoho Learn or digital forms) for new hires to confirm completion of process and safety SOPs. F-06 P3 Low Provides verifiable compliance trails for factory safety and quality audits.
