Section 01 — Executive Summary

Report Overview — Seed

The Seed vertical is an institutional B2B sales division focused on pan-India delivery of polymer-based seed coatings and soil additives to seed companies, universities, and agricultural institutions. Sales are entirely demo-led — requiring on-ground trials before any PO can be raised.

Run entirely by a single BD Manager (Sameer Vishnuji Mahiskar), the vertical operates under severe capacity constraints. Sameer spends 15–20 days a month on physical travel, leaving minimal time for lead generation, which currently relies on manual ChatGPT and Google News searches. With no CRM, follow-ups depend on memory and local ad-hoc spreadsheets. Additionally, proposal creation is bottlenecked by an unstructured R&D data dependency — each pitch requires Sameer to manually extract trial data from R&D's shared Google Drive.

Section 03 — Process Detail

Current Workflows

Analysis of current operational sequences and inter-departmental handoffs

Lead Sourcing & Qualification — P-03

What is good: The warm intro approach respects the relationship-first nature of institutional seed sales. Sameer's on-ground credibility helps open doors that cold outreach cannot.

What needs to be fixed: Lead generation relies on speculative, manual ChatGPT and Google News queries with weak signal. Lead volume is low and intent quality is unverified before outreach.

Bottlenecks: Lead sourcing happens only during the 8–10 non-travel days per month, capping top-of-funnel inflow. No scoring or segmentation exists.

Dependencies: No external data feed. All discovery depends on Sameer's manual search effort and individual memory of past conversations.

Demo & Sales Lifecycle — P-01 P-04

What is good: The in-person demo approach builds credibility that cold outreach cannot replicate. Sowing-window alignment respects the agronomic reality of seed sales.

What needs to be fixed: No CRM means follow-up relies on memory across 25–30 active leads while traveling 15–20 days a month. A missed seasonal window costs 6–12 months of opportunity.

Bottlenecks: The entire 90 MT pan-India target rests on one person — the vertical has zero coverage if Sameer is unavailable. Sample dispatch depends on Logistics hitting precise sowing timelines with no formal SLA.

Dependencies: Logistics must dispatch samples within the institution-specific sowing window. R&D must provide structured trial data for proposal creation. Both are informal.

BD (Seed) Audit Report · v1.0Page 5

Proposal Creation & R&D Data Extraction — P-04 P-07

What is good: Each proposal is tailored to the specific client's crop, region, and trial context — reflecting genuine consultative selling.

What needs to be fixed: Proposal creation takes 1–2.5 days because R&D trial data must be manually extracted from an unstructured Google Drive. Sameer must identify the relevant trial, extract figures, and reformat them into Canva/PPT for each unique client pitch.

Bottlenecks: Manual multi-step process with no template library. If a competitor responds to an institution first with a credible proposal, EFP loses the deal regardless of product quality.

Dependencies: R&D must provide structured trial data (crop type, dose, yield uplift, certificate) in pitch-ready format. Currently informal with no SLA.

PO to Collections Workflow — P-08

What is good: PO intake and production handoff exist as informal but functional steps.

What needs to be fixed: No tested collections workflow exists yet. The vertical has not closed enough volume to have a proven receivables loop with Accounts.

Bottlenecks: Once POs start flowing in volume, the lack of a structured collections process will create reconciliation gaps with Finance.

Dependencies: Production must fulfill on spec; Logistics must deliver on time; Accounts must reconcile invoices — none currently formalized with BD.

Section 06 — Corrective Action Plan

Recommended Solution

This section outlines the integrated technology and process solution proposed to resolve operational bottlenecks, replacing the legacy recommendations register.

Implementation Required

Solution Modules to be Built:

1
Custom React CRM & Pipeline Dashboard
  • Centralised Pipeline Tracking: Build a custom CRM on React JS (unified with all other BD verticals for centralized data) to log all 25–30 active leads with stage-based visibility. Replaces local spreadsheets and memory-reliant follow-up.
  • Automated Follow-Up Cadences: The CRM automatically pings Sameer on follow-up timing, eliminating dropped leads during the 15–20 travel days each month.
  • Seasonal Window Alerts: Sowing-window deadlines are logged as hard SLAs in the system, with automated reminders triggered 2 weeks before the window closes.
2
R&D Data Integration & Proposal Engine
  • Structured Trial Case-Study Cards: R&D trial certificates mapped to standardized, pitch-ready templates (crop type, dose, yield uplift, certificate) that BD can slot directly into proposals.
  • AI Proposal Drafting Engine: A custom assistant that reads existing past proposals and templates stored on Google Drive, generating new proposals in the required format.
  • Unified Dashboard Integration: The proposal engine lives inside the custom React CRM dashboard so Sameer doesn't have to jump between multiple tools while on the road.
3
Automated Communications & Cross-Department SLAs
  • Logistics Sample Tracking: Real-time shipment tracker link for each BD sample dispatch, integrated into the CRM with sowing-window SLA enforcement.
  • Reporting Alignment: Formalize a single primary reporting executive (COO or CBDO) with consolidated KPIs and weekly check-ins.
  • Unified OS Integration: Integrates into the larger EF Polymer operating system, giving CXOs role-based, one-click visibility into pipeline health and BD ROI.

What Remains the Same

  • No Forced Tool Migration: Google Drive remains the source of R&D trial files; the integration layer pulls structured data from it.
  • Relationship-First Selling: Sameer's on-ground demo approach is preserved — the system removes admin load, not field presence.

Tech Stack Utilized

  • CRM: Custom React JS Build (Unified BD OS)
  • Frontend Dashboard: ReactJS + TypeScript
  • APIs: Google Drive, WhatsApp, Gmail, Custom API
  • AI: Gemini/LLMs for proposal drafting
  • Host: Google Cloud Platform

Key Operational Problems Solved

  • Eliminates memory-reliant follow-up across 25–30 active leads during 15–20 travel days per month.
  • Reduces proposal turnaround from 1–2.5 days to hours via structured R&D case-study cards and AI drafting.
  • Provides CXOs real-time pipeline visibility and BD ROI tracking without manual spreadsheet exports.
  • Formalizes Logistics sample-dispatch SLAs to protect seasonal sowing windows.

Future Scope of Improvement / Enhancement

  • Lead Intelligence Engine: In the future, an AI agent could actively monitor tender portals, university bulletins, and seed company expansion news to auto-populate the pipeline with pre-scored, high-intent leads. This is not needed right now but is a planned future enhancement once the South India executive is onboarded.

Next Steps for Team Adoption

  • Mandatory CRM Training: Sameer must be trained on the custom React CRM adoption and follow-up cadence configuration before the next travel cycle.
  • R&D Alignment Workshop: A cross-departmental workshop with Ritu Panwar to define the structured case-study card format and handoff SLA.
  • Feedback Loop: Establish a continuous feedback loop to iterate on the solution on the ground, ensuring it adapts to the realities of field travel.
