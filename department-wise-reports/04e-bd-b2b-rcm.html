Section 01 — Executive Summary

Vertical Overview — B2B & RCM

Scope, current state, and headline findings

The B2B vertical manages major corporate accounts and white-label agreements, primarily anchored by RCM. The operation involves supplying bulk raw material to a central warehouse, which is then distributed by RCM down to ~10,000 retail stores. A secondary channel via Seagreen (broker) is also managed, where EFP uniquely bears the cost of tertiary liquidation.

Led by Jatin Jain, the 7-person team manages a combined regional target of 40 MT across 8 focus states (HR, UP, PB). The most critical operational failure is a complete lack of tertiary sales visibility leading to inventory blind spots. Field training data is recorded in physical paper diaries, causing transcription delays, and 90% of internal and external communication relies on personal WhatsApp numbers.

Section 02 — Risk & Control Findings

Findings Register

Ranked by risk rating. Each finding is cross-referenced to its recommendation in Section 04.
IDFindingRiskEvidence / ImpactLinked Rec.
F-01Tertiary Sales Invisibility. EFP has zero data visibility below the RCM regional depot tier (i.e., the ~10,000 retail stores).HighCreates a massive inventory planning blind spot; demand forecasting is purely reactive.R-01
F-02Manual Paper-Based Field Data. Field training logs and farmer data are written in physical diaries and manually transcribed into Excel.HighHigh risk of data entry error, significant time waste, and delayed billing/reporting.R-02

The cross-departmental supply chain is highly vulnerable to the tertiary data blind spot. Because EFP relies on reactive threshold alerts (when a depot hits 40kg) rather than predictive point-of-sale data from RCM's stores, Production and Logistics are forced into rapid, ad-hoc fulfillment cycles to maintain the 20 MT commitment. Integrating RCM's tertiary sales data directly into EFP's Production planning systems is a critical priority for supply chain stabilization.
