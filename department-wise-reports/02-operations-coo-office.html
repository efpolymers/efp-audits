Section 01 — Executive Summary

Department Overview

Scope, current state, and headline findings

The COO Office manages broad strategic oversight encompassing Finance, HR, Marketing, Retail Sales compliance, and MENA business development. While direct execution is delegated, the COO actively dictates marketing projections, structures Board/Investor reporting, and resolves daily operational friction.

The review uncovered a severe communication bottleneck: managing 50–80 daily emails and 150+ WhatsApp groups has created a saturation point, relying perilously on "unread" email markers for task triage. Additionally, the lack of a dedicated Marketing Manager leaves budgets unmeasured for ROI, and the Retail Sales tracking app fails in low-network regions, bleeding critical compliance data.

Section 02 — Risk & Control Findings

Findings Register

Ranked by risk rating. Each finding is cross-referenced to its recommendation in Section 04.
IDFindingRiskEvidence / ImpactLinked Rec.
F-01 Executive Communication Saturation. COO manages 50–80 emails/day using "unread" filters and 151 shared WhatsApp groups with single staff members. Critical Creates a massive operational bottleneck for approvals; tasks are easily lost; post-10 PM ideas are forgotten entirely. R-01
F-02 Field Tracking & Compliance Leakage. The Retail Sales mobile app fails in low-network agricultural regions. Medium Ground-level compliance gaps; sales pipeline status and qualitative farmer feedback are not reliably recorded. R-02
F-03 Marketing Accountability Gap. No dedicated Marketing Manager; budget execution is handled as a side-task by BD staff. Medium Execution lag post-budget approval; 25-50% of revenue is spent on marketing with zero ROI or conversion tracking. R-03
F-04 Lost Decision Opportunities. Operational ideas conceived outside standard hours lack a capture mechanism. Low Creative friction and forgotten strategic follow-ups due to disorganized tracking. R-01
One of four findings is rated Critical — executive communication saturation (F-01) has created a single-point bottleneck where the COO triages 50–80 emails daily using "unread" markers. This is a shared-resource constraint: it cannot be solved by adding tools alone and requires structural delegation. The Medium findings (F-02, F-03) are operationally distinct — field tracking is a technology gap while marketing accountability is a staffing gap. Phase 1 targets the communication bottleneck as a zero-cost quick win before the heavier field-app and hiring work in Phases 2–3.

Section 03 — Process Detail

Current Workflows

Where each finding sits inside the operational process

Board & Investor Reporting Prep

To insulate operational sales personnel from admin tasks, the COO absorbs the heavy lifting of drafting investor decks from raw, unstructured verbal inputs, adding to the communication saturation (F-01).

MENA Pipeline Lifecycle

Overseeing this MENA cycle requires a HubSpot-style CRM tracking parent Companies and nested Deal/Crop Accounts, currently missing in ops.

Retail Field Tracking & Compliance — F-02

The field app's inability to gracefully handle low-network zones forces reps back to unstructured WhatsApp updates, destroying compliance data.

Marketing Budget Execution — F-03

Operating without a dedicated Marketing Manager leads to significant lags between budget approval and campaign launch, with zero measurement of conversion metrics.
