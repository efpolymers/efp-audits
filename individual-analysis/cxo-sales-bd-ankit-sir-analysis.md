# Operational Documentation: Sales & Business Development (CBDO Office)

## Department Snapshot

### Time & Effort Split
* **Executive Pipeline Review & Meetings:** ~30% (estimated)
* **Strategic Alignment & Vertical Coordination:** ~30% (estimated)
* **Travel Plan Verification & Activity Audits:** ~20% (estimated)
* **Cross-Department & Operations Coordination:** ~20% (estimated)

### Tool Stack
* **Tracking & Pipelines:** Local Excel framework (Cocopeat only), no active database for domestic verticals
* **Comms & Collaboration:** WhatsApp (primary), Slack, Gmail, phone calls

### Key Frequency & Volume Metrics
* **Reporting Cadence:** Monthly status reviews (retrospective; stated directly)
* **Business Development Verticals:** **4** Domestic + **1** New Business vertical (stated directly)
* **Real-time Pipeline Check:** Manual (requires one-to-one meetings; stated directly)

### Red Flags
1. **High**: *Absence of Central CRM* — Lead status and ownership are completely invisible at the executive level, requiring manual meetings to determine account owners or deal stages, leading to ownership leakage.
2. **Medium**: *Retrospective Monthly Reporting* — Pipeline reporting occurs retrospectively once a month, preventing timely CBDO intervention on stalled deals.
3. **Medium**: *Siloed WhatsApp Communications* — Customer interactions and pipeline history live inside employees' personal WhatsApp and email accounts with no central capture, risking lead leakage.
4. **Low**: *Process Disparities* — The Cocopeat division uses a basic tracking system, while domestic verticals have no formal pipeline logging, creating inconsistent tracking discipline.

## 3. Outreach & Lead Sourcing Strategy
* **Vertical-Specific Sourcing:** The CBDO office has rejected a unified lead generation process. Each vertical (Seed, NGO, RCM, Government) operates a distinct lead-generation workflow tailored to its specific target demographic.
* **Lead Sourcing Role Model:** Sourcing tasks are allocated to dedicated lead-generation roles within the existing teams. Lead lists and market insights are shared during regular cross-vertical alignment meetings.

---

## 4. Sales Operations & Pipeline Management
* **Pipeline Status Visibility:** Real-time visibility into active pipelines is limited. Account ownership, current deal stages, and interaction histories are not aggregated in a central system, requiring manual check-ins.
* **Reporting Cadence:** Performance is tracked via retrospective monthly status reports. Verticals categorize leads by sales stage and type to present to CBDO leadership.
* **CRM Implementation Targets (Phase 1):** Implementing a CRM is the primary operational priority. Phase 1 targets include:
  * Consolidated database of all leads and active account owners.
  * Real-time tracking of deal progression stages.
  * Activity logging to verify communication frequency without manual follow-up.
* **Travel Tracking:** Daily travel plans are logged for administrative purposes. No senior executive approval is required for travel execution.

---

## 5. Tooling & Information Infrastructure
* **Pipeline Infrastructure:** No central CRM or system of record is active for legacy BD verticals. Client details are shared via email and WhatsApp.
* **New Business Development Tracking:** A basic tracking framework has been implemented in the Cocopeat vertical. This framework is under evaluation for roll-out to legacy BD verticals.
