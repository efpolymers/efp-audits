1. Executive Summary

1.1 Audit Scope & Approach

This report consolidates findings from 15 department-level operational audits conducted across EF Polymers (EFP), a bio-polymer manufacturing company specializing in agricultural soil-moisture-retention products and cocopeat growing media. The audit covered the entire organizational value chain — from CEO and CXO offices through R&D, Production, Logistics, Finance, Sales, all five Business Development verticals (Cocopeat, Seed, NGO/CSR, Government, B2B/RCM), MENA & International Sales, Marketing & Retail Sales, and HR & Talent Management.

The methodology applied follows a six-layer thinking model (Understanding → Relationships → Constraints → Failure → Optimization → Transformation) with evidence-backed conclusions rated by confidence level.

1.3 Critical Observations

  1. No Central CRM exists. All 5 BD verticals + MENA + Marketing/Retail operate on disconnected spreadsheets, WhatsApp, and personal memory. CBDO confirmed this is the single largest operational risk; every department report independently flagged CRM absence as a critical gap.
  2. Spreadsheet Dependency is systemic. An estimated 30+ independent Excel/Google Sheets serve as primary operational databases across the organization, with zero integration.
  3. Key-Person Dependencies are pervasive. R&D (Ritu Panwar — sole protocol authority), Logistics (Nihal Paliwal — shared across domestic + international), Finance (Shobha Shekhawat), BD-Seed (Sameer Mahiskar — solo pan-India operator), and CEO/COO offices each represent single points of failure for critical business functions.
  4. WhatsApp is the de facto ERP. Order intake, field tracking, payment confirmations, customer communication, PO submissions, attendance verification, and even task management flow through WhatsApp groups with no data capture or audit trail. Marketing alone processes 10,000+ daily WhatsApp updates manually.
  5. COGS is unreliable. Untraceable COGS margin volatility due to absent WIP valuation, no grade-based costing, and unallocated packaging/depreciation costs renders financial reporting unreliable for investor confidence.
  6. Personal device dependency creates institutional risk. ~90% of BD-B2B/RCM and MENA communications run on personal WhatsApp numbers and SIMs. Employee turnover guarantees total loss of contact history and community access.
  7. HR operates without a unified ATS. Recruitment sourcing spans 6+ platforms with no centralized tracking, 30–45 day closing rates, and no AI pre-screening layer.
