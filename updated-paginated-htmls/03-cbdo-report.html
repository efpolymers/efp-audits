Section 01 — Executive Summary

Report Overview

The CBDO manages strategic growth across 4 legacy domestic verticals (Seed, B2B/Company, NGO/CSR, Government) and 1 New Business vertical (Cocopeat & Japan Exports). The team is highly specialized, driving sophisticated institutional sales cycles.

However, the audit reveals critical operational vulnerabilities: the complete absence of a central CRM, siloed product/vertical knowledge leading to prolonged training times, and manual travel approvals that delay field execution. Active deal pipelines and client histories are siloed within the personal WhatsApp accounts of individual executives. The CBDO relies on manual meetings, retrospective monthly reports, and email approval trails to coordinate with Finance. Implementing a custom unified CRM (with direct WhatsApp API lead insertion), a digital product knowledge hub (leveraging the Creative Upaay chatbot), and multichannel approval bots (Slack/WhatsApp) feeding a live CXO Level Dashboard is the urgent priority to secure data, accelerate adoption, and streamline field operations.

Section 03 — Current Workflows & Process Maps

Current Workflows

Organizational Structure & Verticals

What needs to be fixed: The CBDO office manages two distinct divisions: legacy domestic sales and new business/exports. Because these divisions operate with disjointed tracking, corporate oversight is severely fragmented.

Detailed Structure:
• Legacy Domestic BD: Comprises Seed/Contract Farming (Sameer), B2B - SeaGreen & RCM (Jatin), NGO/CSR (Vikas), and Government Sales (Shailendra).
• New BD & Exports: Focuses on Cocopeat (Mohit & Aishwarya) and B2B Japan Exports (Yoshida-san).

Decentralized Lead Generation Strategy

What needs to be fixed: Centralized lead generation failed due to distinct vertical-specific buyer personas. However, the current decentralized model operates with zero shared intelligence, risking parallel or conflicting approaches.

Detailed Workflow Steps:
1. Decentralization: Each of the 4 domestic verticals owns its lead generation strategy independently.
2. Dedicated Agents: BD executives focus exclusively on vertical-specific pitching and sourcing.
3. Coordination: The CBDO facilitates periodic info-sharing meetings to align targets and prevent cross-vertical lead collision.

Report Overview · EF PolymerPage 5

Lead Tracking Bottleneck — P-01 P-03

What needs to be fixed: Because deals are tracked entirely in personal apps, the CBDO cannot intervene to rescue stalled deals until they appear dead on a month-end report.

Detailed Workflow Steps:
1. Lead Generation: Leads are acquired via regional networks, dealer channels, or direct inquiry.
2. Information Silo: Deal details, client correspondence, and stage status are stored exclusively in executives' personal WhatsApp chats.
3. Manual Reconciliation: The CBDO must hold intensive 1:1 meetings with each BD executive to manually audit pipeline stages.
4. Retrospective View: A static, retrospective spreadsheet report is compiled and submitted at the end of each month.

Vertical Tracking Disparity — P-04

What needs to be fixed: The disparity in basic data-entry discipline creates friction when attempting to synthesize a holistic company-wide pipeline view for the CBDO.

Detailed Workflow Steps:
1. Cocopeat Division: Utilizes a basic Excel spreadsheet tracker for pipeline stages.
2. Legacy BD Divisions: Seed, B2B, NGO, and Govt verticals have no formal pipeline tracking.
3. Friction: Cross-vertical synthesis is impossible because of incompatible tracking formats.
4. Review Limitation: CBDO pipeline reviews remain qualitative, ad hoc, and inconsistent.

Cross-Department Dependencies

Finance & Accounts
Sales-to-Finance Data Sync & Travel Workflow
Operations Dependency
Finance must stop manual reconciliation of invoicing and payment records. The BD team needs Zoho Books billing, receivables, and payment confirmations to sync automatically into the central CRM. Furthermore, Finance must connect with the new multichannel travel approval bot to handle immediate reimbursement payouts upon CBDO/vertical head approval.
👤 Key Person: Shobha Shekhawat (Finance Head)
Critical Bottleneck
Without automated payment sync and multichannel travel approval flows, BD executives must manually query Finance to verify payment status before dispatches, and travel payouts face long delays due to manual email trails.
Data Transfer & Updates Needed
Configure automated Zoho Books-to-CRM API feeds to update customer account credit limits, payment history, and outstanding invoices, and establish Slack/WhatsApp automated webhook triggers for travel and reimbursement approvals.
Technical & Field Operations
Product Testing & Field Issues SLA
Operations Dependency
The BD team frequently encounters crop-specific field issues (e.g., product performance complaints, software-related queries from farmers) and depends on the Technical team for resolution. Additionally, the BD team needs live visibility into which product formulations have completed testing and are ready for commercial sale.
👤 Key Person: Ritu Panwar (R&D Lead)
Critical Bottleneck
Lack of visibility into testing completion leads to the risk of BD executives pitching unvalidated formulations, and unresolved field complaints persist for weeks, severely damaging distributor and client trust.
Data Transfer & Updates Needed
The Technical team must publish a live product testing register and formulation roadmap inside the CRM, and establish a formal field-issue escalation SLA (e.g., 24-hour triage, 72-hour resolution) integrated with deal records.

Section 06 — Recommended Solution

Recommended Solution

This section outlines the integrated technology solution proposed to resolve operational bottlenecks, replacing the legacy recommendations register.

Implementation Required

Solution Modules to be Built:

1
Custom Unified CRM & Lead Ownership
  • Custom CRM Build: Build a custom unified CRM tailored for all 5 verticals (Seed, B2B, NGO, Govt, Cocopeat) as per their specific working style and data-capture needs.
  • WhatsApp API Lead Insertion: Connect the custom CRM directly with the WhatsApp API to allow executives to manually insert new leads and updates via WhatsApp directly.
  • Vertical Autonomy: Set up distinct layout views and custom pipelines for each vertical to ensure smooth workflow adoption without cross-vertical clutter.
2
Live CXO Level Dashboard
  • Real-Time Pipeline View: Shift from manual monthly retrospective reviews to a live CXO Level Dashboard monitoring pipeline health daily.
  • Stalled Deal Alerts: Automated alerts flag any deal with no activity for 7+ days directly on the CXO Dashboard.
  • Conversion Analytics: Track deal velocity, conversion ratios, and cross-vertical benchmarking in real time.
  • Lead Approached vs. Converted: Daily count of leads approached and leads converted, visible on the CXO Level Dashboard for immediate performance monitoring.
3
Cross-Vertical Standardization
  • Unified Tracker Framework: Deploy the Cocopeat vertical's Excel tracking framework across all 4 legacy domestic verticals with unified column definitions as a stop-gap before CRM launch.
  • Standardized Pipeline Stages: Define uniform stage labels (Prospect, Qualified, Proposal, Negotiation, Closed) to align cross-vertical reporting and build data-entry discipline.
4
Central Product & CRM Knowledge Hub
  • Single Source of Truth: Digital repository containing crop specs, product formulas, and vertical personas. Leverages the existing 60-70% built AI chatbot developed by Creative Upaay.
  • Adoption is the Challenge: Since the knowledge layer is largely built, the primary roadblock is team adoption. Standard workflows must mandate querying the bot.
  • CRM SOP playbooks: Incorporate interactive data-entry guidelines and chatbot queries into training materials to accelerate onboarding.
Report Overview · EF PolymerPage 12
5
Multichannel CXO Approvals & Travel Bot
  • Unified Approval Feed: Consolidated dashboard section displaying all pending travel plans, client meeting schedules, and expense reimbursement requests from the BD team.
  • Multichannel Actions: Integrates Slack and WhatsApp bots, allowing vertical heads and the CBDO to review and click-to-approve requests instantly on-the-go.
  • Auto-Logging & Sync: Approved travel logs update executive schedules automatically (without daily manual planning approval needed).
  • Direct Finance Routing: Click-to-approve actions automatically trigger payment/booking notifications straight to Shobha Shekhawat (Finance) for instant disbursement.

What Remains the Same

  • Sales Methodology: The BD team's relationship-driven approach remains unchanged; the custom CRM digital tool supports rather than replaces it.
  • Vertical Autonomy: Each vertical retains its operational independence within the custom CRM framework.

Tech Stack Utilized

  • CRM: Custom Unified CRM (React + Python/Node)
  • Dashboard: ReactJS + TypeScript (CXO Dashboard)
  • Integration: WhatsApp API (Manual Lead Insertion), Slack API, Zoho Books API
  • Knowledge Layer: Creative Upaay AI Chatbot (60-70% built) / Central Wiki

Key Operational Problems Solved

  • Eliminates lead leakage risk during employee turnover by securing corporate ownership of all contact data.
  • Replaces monthly retrospective reviews with live pipeline visibility on a CXO Level Dashboard.
  • Enables manual lead insertion directly via WhatsApp, saving time and keeping communications aligned.
  • Standardizes tracking discipline across all 5 BD verticals, eliminating cross-vertical reporting friction.
  • Provides daily travel plan visibility and lead approach-vs-conversion metrics for immediate executive oversight.
  • Tackles team training and tech adoption by leveraging the 60-70% built Creative Upaay AI chatbot as a product and CRM knowledge hub.
  • Eliminates manual delays in travel and expense approvals via instant Slack/WhatsApp direct approvals.
  • Automates flight approval routing through CBDO → Accounts, while daily travel plans remain auto-logged without approval.

Future Scope of Improvement / Enhancement

  • AI Deal Scoring: In the future, an AI model could analyze CRM data to score deal health, predict win probability, and recommend next-best-actions for each BD executive. This is not needed right now but is a planned future enhancement.

Next Steps for Team Adoption

  • Mandatory CRM & Product Chatbot Training: All BD executives must undergo compulsory training focusing on product vertical knowledge and CRM usage, actively querying the 60-70% built Creative Upaay AI chatbot.
  • Feedback Loop: Establish weekly 15-minute pipeline stand-ups to instill data-entry discipline and iterate on the custom CRM workflow design based on ground feedback.
  • Change Management & Tech Adoption Focus: The CBDO will actively enforce CRM compliance. Since adoption is the main challenge (P-05), top-down enforcement (incentives linked to CRM and chatbot queries) is critical for success.
