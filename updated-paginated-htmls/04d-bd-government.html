Section 01 — Executive Summary

Report Overview — Government, B2B & Startups

Scope, current state, and headline findings

The Government & B2B vertical manages Pan-India government-sponsored crop trials (e.g., Uttarakhand Govt Project), institutional B2B accounts, and startup collaborations. It is responsible for tracking billing compliance, collecting Pradhan and Farmer data from the ground, preparing Demand Drafts, processing new orders, and maintaining custom trial tracking software built in collaboration with an external developer.

Operated primarily by BD Executive Shailendra Singh and team (reporting to CBDO Ankit Jain), the vertical suffers from severe workflow isolation. Daily operations, task assignments, PO sharing, invoice handoffs, field media collection, and travel reimbursements all run exclusively through WhatsApp. There is no tracking dashboard for logistics, no centralized data store for Farmer/Pradhan records, and no automated calendar or HR visibility — creating compounding bottlenecks across Accounts, Logistics, HR, and Marketing.

Section 04 — Process Detail

Current Workflows

Analysis of current operational sequences

Government Trials & Data Collection Workflow

What is good: Field staff consistently capture media at the individual Pradhan and executive level, and the team demonstrates a genuine willingness to adopt structured technology solutions to improve field workflows.

What needs to be fixed: The manual "WhatsApp-to-Drive" process creates a massive administrative burden. Individual-level photos are stored per Pradhan and per executive team member — making this even more granular and time-consuming than project-level uploads. Task management is entirely unstructured, living in WhatsApp threads.

Bottlenecks: Lack of automated HR sync for travel/leave causes scheduling conflicts — worsened by network unavailability in remote field areas. A documentation gap also exists: Demand Drafts are submitted via three distinct methods, but only one has been captured in this audit.

Detailed Workflow Steps:
1. On-Ground Activity: Field staff conduct government trials, capture Pradhan/Farmer data and photos.
2. WhatsApp Relay: Photos and data are shared via WhatsApp to BD Executive (Shailendra Singh).
3. Manual Drive Upload: BD Executive manually downloads photos and uploads them to per-Pradhan/per-executive folders on Google Drive.
4. Database Entry: Farmer profiles, billings, and Pradhan data are compiled into shared Google Sheets.
5. CBDO Review: Tracker and billings are shared with CBDO (Ankit Jain) for review and sign-off.

Cross-Department Involvement: Field Staff (On-ground data capture), Shailendra Singh (BD Executive) (WhatsApp relay & manual Drive upload), CBDO Office / Ankit Jain (Review & task assignment), and Marketing / Vandana Jain (Design collateral on project basis).

Dependencies & Bottlenecks: Invoice handoffs to Accounts and Logistics are manual and prone to human error. Design collateral for government events is routed to Vandana and retrieved from Google Drive without a structured DAM.

Order Processing & Logistics Workflow — F-03 F-05

What is good: A clear sequence exists — PO receipt triggers the payment and dispatch chain, showing an awareness of interdependencies across BD, Accounts, and Logistics.

What needs to be fixed: The entire handoff relies on WhatsApp and phone calls. There is no single shared platform for BD to submit POs, Accounts to confirm payments, or Logistics to push tracking updates.

Bottlenecks: BD team members must manually call Logistics every time they need a status update on a shipment. Travel reimbursements follow the same WhatsApp-only pattern — compiled and submitted manually at month end.

Detailed Workflow Steps:
1. Order Initiation: BD receives Purchase Order (PO) from government client.
2. WhatsApp Handoff: BD shares PO and invoice details with Accounts via WhatsApp call.
3. Payment Clearance: Accounts team confirms payment clearance (no shared platform).
4. Dispatch: Logistics dispatches items once payment is confirmed.
5. Tracking Follow-up: BD manually calls Logistics for shipment status updates.

Cross-Department Involvement: Shailendra Singh (BD Executive) (PO intake & WhatsApp relay), Finance & Accounts (Shobha Shekhawat — Payment confirmation), Logistics (Dispatch & tracking), and CBDO Office (Reimbursement review).

Dependencies & Bottlenecks: Dispatch is blocked until Accounts confirms payment. BD has no visibility into this confirmation except through manual follow-up. A unified Accounts + Logistics form with a WhatsApp bot for status queries would resolve this end-to-end.

Section 07 — Corrective Action Plan

Recommended Solution Segment

This section outlines the integrated technology solution proposed to resolve operational bottlenecks across the Government BD vertical.

Implementation Required

Solution Modules to be Built:

1
BD Operations Command Centre
  • Centralised Task Board: Migrate all CBDO task assignments from WhatsApp into a persistent Zoho Projects board. Every task is timestamped, auditable, and cannot be lost in a chat thread.
  • Unified PO & Invoice Flow: BD submits Purchase Orders once via a shared form. Accounts confirms payment and Logistics updates dispatch status — all visible to BD in real time without any manual follow-up calls.
  • WhatsApp Bot Integration: Connect the dashboard to a WhatsApp bot so team members can instantly query package tracking status or invoice details without calling Accounts or Logistics.
2
HR & Attendance Sync
  • Auto-Sync Calendar: Google Calendar integrated on BD team mobile devices so travel plans and leave entries update automatically — bypassing the network constraints in remote field areas.
  • Real-Time HR Dashboard: HR gains a live visibility panel showing field availability across the BD team, eliminating scheduling conflicts caused by missed manual updates.
  • Centralised Reimbursements: Travel invoices uploaded once to the shared platform; amounts are auto-fetched by Accounts, eliminating the manual month-end compilation cycle.
3
Field Data & Media Automation
  • Mobile Photo Upload Forms: Field staff submit Pradhan and executive-level photos directly to the correct Google Drive project folders via a mobile form — eliminating the WhatsApp download-and-re-upload loop entirely.
  • Demand Draft SOP Completion: A dedicated follow-up audit session will document all three Demand Draft submission methods, and an automated SOP will be built for each to ensure compliance.
4
Farmer & Pradhan CRM
  • Replace Manual Google Sheets: A dedicated CRM to securely store all Farmer and Pradhan records — searchable by project, billing amount, district, and date — protecting institutional data and enabling historical analysis.
  • Government Project Targeting: Structured historical records allow the BD team to identify repeat-trial candidates and build a data-driven pipeline for future government projects.

5
AI & Knowledge Systems
  • DAM via RAG Bot: All marketing collaterals — government event banners, posters, presentation decks — stored on Google Drive are connected to a RAG bot on the company dashboard. BD team members search and retrieve specific assets via natural language queries, removing the bottleneck of contacting Vandana for every request.
  • Unified OS Integration: All modules integrate into the larger EF Polymer operating system, giving CBDO and department heads role-based, real-time visibility across all BD activities in one place.

What Remains the Same

  • No New Backend Database: Google Sheets and Drive remain the core data sources — the new CRM layers on top of them.
  • Familiar Communication Channels: WhatsApp remains the frontline channel, but is now bot-enabled and structured.

Tech Stack Utilized

  • Frontend: ReactJS + TypeScript (Dashboard)
  • APIs: Google Sheets, Drive, WhatsApp Bot, Zoho
  • AI: Gemini / LLMs for RAG (Marketing Asset Bot)
  • Host: Google Cloud Platform

Key Operational Problems Solved

  • Eliminates all manual PO, invoice, and logistics handoffs — replaced by a single shared dashboard and WhatsApp bot.
  • Removes the WhatsApp photo download-and-re-upload loop — saves ~10 hrs/week of field admin time.
  • Protects institutional Farmer and Pradhan data in a secure, searchable CRM instead of unstructured Google Sheets.
  • Provides HR real-time field availability and removes scheduling conflicts caused by remote-area network constraints.

Future Scope of Improvement / Enhancement

  • AI Lead Scoring: In the future, the Farmer/Pradhan CRM can be extended with an AI layer that scores government project leads by historical trial success rates and district crop patterns, enabling proactive targeting rather than reactive follow-up.

Next Steps for Team Adoption

  • Mandatory Training: All BD team members must complete compulsory training sessions on the new dashboard, CRM, and WhatsApp bot before go-live. Training is non-optional.
  • Feedback Loop: Establish a 30-day post-launch feedback cycle to iterate on the solution based on ground-level usage, ensuring genuine adoption rather than tool abandonment.
